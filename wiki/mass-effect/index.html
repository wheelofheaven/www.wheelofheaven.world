Mass Effect

The Mass Effect, also called the Law of Masstime, is the framework concept developed in the Wheel of Heaven corpus to articulate the relationship between mass-or-scale and the experience of time across the infinite hierarchy of cosmological scales. The concept holds that the subjective experience of time scales with mass and complexity: a larger and more massive being experiences each subjective moment as encompassing a substantially longer interval of absolute time than a smaller being's subjective moment encompasses. The law's principal source-material warrant is the Yahweh-delivered passage in Extra-Terrestrials Took Me to Their Planet (1975), which establishes the framework in connection with the broader Infinity framework and the Cosmic Chain framework. The Mass Effect framework has substantial points of contact with mainstream physics (general-relativistic gravitational time dilation, biological time perception, allometric scaling laws) while extending substantially beyond what mainstream science has established.

The Mass Effect, also called the Law of Masstime, is the framework concept developed in the Wheel of Heaven corpus to articulate the relationship between mass-or-scale and the experience of time across the infinite hierarchy of cosmological scales. The framework holds that the subjective experience of time scales with mass and complexity: a larger and more massive being experiences each subjective moment as encompassing a substantially longer interval of absolute time than a smaller being's subjective moment encompasses. Equivalently, smaller beings experience proportionally more subjective time per unit absolute time than larger beings — a eukaryotic cell, an ant, a human, an elephant, the Earth itself, and the gigantic being of whom the sun is an atom each operate within their own characteristic subjective-time scale, with the scale ratios reflecting the mass-or-complexity ratios across the broader cosmological hierarchy. The law's principal source-material warrant is the Yahweh-delivered passage in Extra-Terrestrials Took Me to Their Planet (1975), which establishes the framework in the broader cosmological context and connects it explicitly to the Infinity framework and the Cosmic Chain framework.

The framework's specific content can be articulated in two equivalent formulations. The first formulation, focusing on subjective time per unit absolute time: the subjective time interval that a being experiences per unit of absolute time is inversely proportional to the being's mass-or-scale, with smaller beings experiencing more subjective time per unit absolute time and larger beings experiencing less. The second formulation, focusing on the absolute time encompassed within a unit of subjective time: the absolute time interval that one of a being's subjective moments encompasses is positively proportional to the being's mass-or-scale, with larger beings' subjective moments encompassing substantially more absolute time than smaller beings' subjective moments encompass. The two formulations are mathematically equivalent — they are alternative ways of articulating the same underlying scaling relationship — but they emphasize different aspects of the framework's content. The Yahweh passage uses the second formulation explicitly: "when a millennium has gone by for you, the gigantic being of whom the sun is an atom has only had the time to take one step." The corpus's broader engagement uses both formulations across various contexts.

The Mass Effect framework has substantial points of contact with mainstream physics and biology. Within mainstream physics, general-relativistic gravitational time dilation establishes that time runs more slowly in stronger gravitational fields — empirically confirmed through the Pound-Rebka experiment (1959), the GPS satellite system's required relativistic corrections, and various atomic-clock experiments at different altitudes. The Mass Effect's specific framework structurally extends gravitational time dilation through the broader cosmological-hierarchy application, treating mass-induced time dilation as operating not only at extreme densities (black holes, neutron stars) but as a general feature of cosmological organization across all scales. Within mainstream biology, allometric scaling laws establish that biological time scales systematically with body mass: Kleiber's law (metabolic rate ∝ mass^(3/4)), the heart-rate scaling law (heart rate ∝ mass^(-1/4)), and the lifespan scaling law (lifespan ∝ mass^(1/4)) all operate consistently with the Mass Effect framework's broader content. The biological time-perception scholarship (Eagleman, Wittmann, and others) has further established that smaller animals perceive time at proportionally faster rates — fluorescent lights appear flickering rather than steady, motion appears slowed, reaction times are proportionally faster. The corpus's framework reads these mainstream scientific findings as confirming the Mass Effect's specific content within the empirical domains where the law has been tested, while the framework's broader cosmological extensions go substantially beyond what mainstream science has established.

The reading is interpretive construction. The Raëlian source material provides explicit articulation in a single Yahweh-delivered passage, but the broader systematic framework that the corpus has developed — the integration with the Cosmic Chain, the formal articulation of the two equivalent formulations, the engagement with mainstream relativity and biological scaling, the cross-cultural comparative material — represents substantial corpus development beyond what the source material directly provides. The framework's epistemic status is methodological-and-empirical rather than mathematically formalized: the corpus does not yet provide a mathematical formalization of the law (no specific equation relating subjective time to mass with calibrated coefficients), but the law's predictive content is substantial across the empirical domains where it can be tested. The corpus registers this epistemic status explicitly: the Mass Effect is one specific framework concept whose mature mathematization remains a future project, but whose qualitative content is well-established through substantial empirical observation across multiple domains.

Etymology and naming

The framework concept has several distinct designations operating across the source material and the corpus's broader treatment.

"Mass Effect" as the principal designation

The English term "Mass Effect" is the corpus's principal designation for the framework. The construction emphasizes the specific causal relationship: the effect of mass on the experience of time. The term is corpus-internal — it does not appear in the Raëlian source material under this specific designation. The term has been adopted by various Raëlian and Raëlism-adjacent communities as a standard designation across the post-1975 period.

"Law of Masstime" as alternative designation

The term "Law of Masstime" is the corpus's alternative designation, emphasizing the lawlike status of the principle and the specific relationship between mass and time. The compound masstime is constructed analogously to spacetime in relativistic physics, registering the specific relationship between mass and time that the framework articulates. The term has been used principally in more technical-philosophical contexts within the broader corpus engagement.

Source-material phrasing

The source material does not use a single technical designation. The principal phrasing is descriptive:

  • "Time is inversely proportional to the mass" — the Yahweh-passage's specific phrasing, emphasizing the inverse-proportionality relationship
  • "The level of the form of life" — the source's specific qualifying phrase, registering that the relationship operates with respect to level of organization rather than purely with respect to mass in the kinematic sense
  • "The time lag due to the enormous difference in mass" — descriptive phrasing for the specific operational consequence

Related conceptual terminology

Several related conceptual terms operate within the framework:

  • "Time lag" — the source's term for the specific operational consequence of the mass-time relationship
  • "Subjective time" — the corpus's term for the time experienced by a being from its own internal perspective
  • "Absolute time" — the corpus's term for the time measured against an external reference frame (with the substantial caveat that "absolute time" is itself a problematic concept within the broader framework, treated below under Modern reinterpretations)
  • "Scale-time correspondence" — the corpus's term for the broader principle that scale and time are systematically related across the cosmological hierarchy

The cross-corpus terminology is consistent in its specific framing: the framework concerns the systematic relationship between mass-or-scale and the experience of time across the broader cosmological hierarchy.

Conventional understanding

The Mass Effect framework as a specific cosmological law is largely a corpus-internal construction. The underlying empirical phenomena (gravitational time dilation, biological time scaling, time perception scaling) are substantially well-established within mainstream science, but the broader Mass Effect framework's specific cosmological-hierarchical formulation extends substantially beyond mainstream scientific articulation.

Mainstream physics

Mainstream physics has produced substantial work on the relationship between mass and time, principally within the framework of general relativity.

Special-relativistic time dilation (Einstein, 1905) establishes that time runs at different rates in different reference frames depending on relative velocity. The specific formula — t' = t·γ where γ = 1/√(1 - v²/c²) — describes the time-dilation factor as a function of relative velocity. Special-relativistic time dilation is empirically confirmed through atomic-clock experiments on aircraft (Hafele-Keating 1971), high-energy particle physics (muon decay rates), and various other tests. The relationship is velocity-based rather than mass-based, distinguishing it from the Mass Effect framework's specific content.

General-relativistic gravitational time dilation (Einstein, 1915) establishes that time runs more slowly in stronger gravitational fields. The specific relationship — clocks at lower gravitational potential run more slowly than clocks at higher gravitational potential — has been empirically confirmed through:

  • The Pound-Rebka experiment (1959) at Harvard demonstrating gravitational redshift across a 22.5-meter vertical column at the Jefferson Physical Laboratory
  • GPS satellite corrections continuously confirming relativistic time dilation as a routine engineering necessity (GPS satellites at altitude run faster than ground clocks by approximately 38 microseconds per day, requiring continuous correction for accurate position determination)
  • Atomic-clock experiments at different altitudes demonstrating the predicted gravitational time-dilation effects with substantial precision (the National Institute of Standards and Technology's 2010 experiment demonstrating gravitational time dilation across height differences as small as 33 centimeters)
  • Black hole and neutron star observations providing extreme gravitational time-dilation environments where the predicted effects become dramatic

The mainstream-relativistic framework establishes that mass produces time dilation, with the relationship being mediated by gravitational potential. The Mass Effect framework's structural overlap with general relativity is substantial — both treat mass as causing time-dilation effects — but the Mass Effect framework's specific content extends substantially beyond what general relativity has established, particularly regarding (a) the role of complexity-or-organization-level rather than purely mass, (b) the application to subjective time and biological-cognitive scales, and (c) the broader cosmological-hierarchical formulation.

The mainstream physics position on absolute vs. relative time. Mainstream physics has substantially abandoned the Newtonian conception of absolute universal time. Within special and general relativity, time is treated as relative — there is no universal time-frame against which all events can be measured, only specific reference frames within which time can be measured. This represents a significant departure from the Newtonian conception (treated more fully under Modern reinterpretations below). The Mass Effect framework operates within this broadly post-Newtonian conception of time, while extending the relativistic framework substantially through the broader cosmological-hierarchical formulation.

Mainstream biology

Mainstream biology has produced substantial work on the relationship between body size and biological time, principally within the framework of allometric scaling laws.

Kleiber's law (Max Kleiber, 1932) establishes that metabolic rate scales as body mass to the power of 3/4: B ∝ M^(3/4). The relationship has been empirically confirmed across substantial portions of the animal kingdom, with various theoretical explanations (the West-Brown-Enquist 1997 fractal-network theory, various other proposals). The implication for biological time: larger animals have proportionally lower metabolic rates per unit mass, with substantial consequences for the broader biological-temporal scaling.

The heart-rate scaling law establishes that heart rate scales as body mass to the power of -1/4: f_heart ∝ M^(-1/4). Larger animals have substantially slower heart rates than smaller animals (an elephant's resting heart rate ≈ 25 bpm; a mouse's resting heart rate ≈ 600 bpm — the ratio reflecting the mass ratio scaled by the appropriate power).

The lifespan scaling law establishes that lifespan scales as body mass to the power of 1/4: T_life ∝ M^(1/4). Larger animals live substantially longer than smaller animals (an elephant's lifespan ≈ 60-70 years; a mouse's lifespan ≈ 2-3 years).

The integrated implication of these scaling laws is that biological time scales systematically with body mass: larger animals have proportionally slower metabolic rates, slower heart rates, and longer lifespans, while smaller animals have proportionally faster rates and shorter lifespans. The total number of heartbeats across a typical lifespan is approximately constant across mammalian species (≈ 10^9), suggesting that biological time itself is scaled to body size in a way that the Mass Effect framework articulates explicitly.

Biological time perception

Mainstream biology and neuroscience have produced substantial work on time perception across species, with principal findings consistent with the Mass Effect framework.

David Eagleman's work (Baylor College of Medicine, then Stanford University) has produced substantial research on time perception, including the empirical demonstration that smaller animals perceive time more slowly per unit absolute time than larger animals. The principal empirical evidence includes critical flicker fusion frequency (the rate at which a flickering light appears steady) — small fast-moving animals (flies, hummingbirds) have substantially higher critical flicker fusion frequencies than larger slower-moving animals (elephants, humans), implying that they perceive the world at proportionally higher temporal resolution.

Marc Wittmann's work has produced substantial research on time perception in humans, with various findings on how subjective time perception varies with cognitive state, age, and various other factors.

The integrated implication: smaller animals not only have proportionally faster biological processes but also perceive time at proportionally higher temporal resolution. The framework's specific content is consistent with this empirical finding while extending it substantially to the broader cosmological-hierarchical scale.

The integrated mainstream position

The integrated mainstream scientific position is that mass and time are systematically related — through gravitational time dilation in physics, through allometric scaling laws in biology, and through time-perception scaling in neuroscience. The various findings collectively establish substantial empirical support for the broad type of relationship the Mass Effect framework articulates, while the framework's specific cosmological-hierarchical extension goes substantially beyond what mainstream science has established. The framework's relationship to mainstream science is one of structural-empirical-consistency-with-substantial-extension.

In primary sources

The framework's principal primary-source material is the Yahweh-delivered passage in Extra-Terrestrials Took Me to Their Planet (1975), with substantial supporting material across the broader source material.

The principal Yahweh passage

The principal source-material passage appears in Extra-Terrestrials Took Me to Their Planet, in the section "Neither God nor Soul" (Book Two of the consolidated Message from the Designers English edition). The passage's full content:

"You also know that we have discovered that there is organized, intelligent life on the level of the infinitely small, quite certainly as evolved as we are and comparable to what we are ourselves. We have been able to prove this."

"From there, we have discovered that the stars and planets are the atoms of a gigantic being, which itself certainly contemplates other stars with curiosity. It is also highly likely that the people living on the infinitely small levels of the infinitely large person and his fellow creatures have known periods when they believed in an immaterial 'God'."

"You must fully understand that everything is in everything. At this moment in an atom of your arm, millions of worlds are being born and others are dying, believing or not believing in a 'God' and a soul, and when a millennium has gone by for you, the gigantic being of whom the sun is an atom has only had the time to take one step."

"Time is, in fact, inversely proportional to the mass, or rather to the level of the form of life. But everything in the universe is alive and in harmony with the infinitely large and the infinitely small."

"The Earth is alive like all the planets, and for the small growth that is humanity, it is difficult to notice this because of the time lag due to the enormous difference in mass, which prevents you from perceiving its palpitations. Nor could one of our red blood cells, or better still, one of the atoms that make up our body imagine that it forms, with its peers, a living being."

"Finally, whatever happens to each individual, the universal balance remains constant."

The passage establishes several interrelated claims:

1. The infinite-hierarchy framework. Intelligent life exists at every scale from the infinitely small through the infinitely large. The framework operates within the broader Infinity framework treated more fully in dedicated entries.

2. The "everything in everything" principle. The fractal-self-similar structure of the cosmos: each scale of organization contains within it the next scale down, with the structure repeating indefinitely. The principle is structurally identical with the Hermetic "as above, so below."

3. The specific Mass Effect law. "Time is, in fact, inversely proportional to the mass, or rather to the level of the form of life." The principal source-material warrant for the framework. The qualifying phrase "or rather to the level of the form of life" is operationally significant — the law is not strictly about mass in the kinematic sense but about the broader level-of-organization that mass approximates within the cosmological hierarchy.

4. The thousand-year-step concrete illustration. "When a millennium has gone by for you, the gigantic being of whom the sun is an atom has only had the time to take one step." The specific quantitative illustration: a thousand human years correspond to approximately one step (perhaps one second of subjective time?) for the gigantic being of whom the sun is an atom. The illustration provides the framework's principal quantitative anchor, registering the substantial scale ratios that the law produces across the cosmological hierarchy.

5. The time-lag operational consequence. "For the small growth that is humanity, it is difficult to notice this because of the time lag due to the enormous difference in mass." The specific operational consequence — beings of substantially different scale cannot easily perceive each other's existence because their characteristic time scales are too disparate.

The "no communication across infinite scales" passage

A subsequent passage in the same section reinforces the framework's specific content with substantial implications for the broader theological-philosophical content:

"There cannot be any communication between an infinitely large entity and a universe of infinitely small entities, because the difference in mass is too great, thus creating a difference in the flow of equivalent time."

The passage establishes that the Mass Effect produces substantial operational consequences for the broader cosmological framework — specifically, that beings of substantially different scales cannot communicate across the scale-difference because the time-scaling differences are too great to permit any meaningful information exchange. This has substantial implications for the framework's broader engagement with traditional theological frameworks (treated below).

Sendy on time

Jean Sendy in his various works (treated more fully in the Jean Sendy entry when written) develops substantial complementary content on time. The principal Sendy positions warrant treatment for their specific philosophical engagement with the Mass Effect framework.

Sendy's specific position is that time is "parasitic to all formulations and equations of physics" and "an arbitrary element." Time has no existence in itself; the notion is subjective and derived from biological and mental organization, projected into the exterior world to create the "irresistible illusion" of an "absolute universal time." Scientific time is conventional, based on physical standards susceptible of being coordinated under different forms in arbitrary ways. The various relativistic formulations that confuse the dimensions of space and time are, on Sendy's reading, "ridiculous" — though Sendy acknowledges that the relativistic framework captures certain genuine empirical phenomena, his broader position is that the time-as-fundamental-dimension treatment of mainstream physics is substantially mistaken.

The framework's relationship to Sendy's specific position is one of substantial overlap with specific extension. The corpus accepts Sendy's broader position that subjective time is the principal reality and that absolute time is an abstraction, while developing the Mass Effect framework's specific content beyond what Sendy's work directly provides.

  • The "no first creator" position of the Cosmic Chain framework (treated more fully in the Cosmic Chain entry)

The concept's content

The basic operational principle

The Mass Effect operates through several interrelated components:

The basic scaling relationship. The subjective experience of time and the mass-or-scale of the experiencing being are systematically related. Specifically:

  • A being of mass-or-scale M, experiencing one unit of subjective time, encompasses an interval of absolute time proportional to M (or to some appropriate function of M)
  • Equivalently, the rate of subjective time per unit absolute time scales inversely with mass-or-scale: dt_subjective/dt_absolute ∝ 1/M
  • The two formulations are mathematically equivalent — they are alternative ways of articulating the same underlying relationship

The qualifying phrase: level of form of life. The source's specific qualifying phrase — "or rather to the level of the form of life" — registers that the relationship operates with respect to level of organization rather than purely with respect to mass in the kinematic sense. A complex biological organism (an elephant) and an inorganic mass of equivalent kinematic mass (a large rock, say) do not necessarily exhibit the same subjective time scale, because the level-of-organization differs substantially. The Mass Effect framework is therefore not a strict mass-vs-time scaling law but a broader complexity-and-organization vs subjective-time law within which mass operates as one significant variable.

The hierarchical scope. The relationship operates across the entire cosmological hierarchy:

  • Subatomic scales — beings smaller than human-scale-atoms presumably experience proportionally faster subjective time than humans
  • Cellular scales — cells experience proportionally faster subjective time than human-scale beings
  • Human scale — the reference scale within which the Wheel of Heaven framework operates
  • Planetary scales — the Earth and other planets experience proportionally slower subjective time than humans (with the source's specific phrasing identifying this through the "Earth's palpitations" and broader living-Earth content)
  • Stellar scales — the gigantic being of whom the sun is an atom experiences substantially slower subjective time than humans (the thousand-years-per-step illustration)
  • Galactic and beyond — successive scales above the stellar level continue the pattern indefinitely

The scaling extends in both directions. The Mass Effect operates symmetrically — subjective time scales faster for smaller beings and slower for larger beings, with the same relationship operating across all scales. The infinite-hierarchy structure (treated in the Infinity entry when written) provides the broader cosmological context within which this symmetric scaling operates.

The two equivalent formulations

The Mass Effect's specific content can be articulated in two mathematically equivalent formulations.

Formulation 1: Subjective time per unit absolute time. The rate at which a being experiences subjective time per unit absolute time is inversely proportional to the being's mass-or-scale. A larger being experiences proportionally less subjective time per unit absolute time; a smaller being experiences proportionally more. Symbolically: r_subjective ∝ 1/M, where r_subjective is the subjective-time-per-unit-absolute-time rate and M is the being's mass-or-scale.

Formulation 2: Absolute time encompassed by a unit of subjective time. The amount of absolute time encompassed within one of a being's subjective time units (say, one second of subjective experience) is positively proportional to the being's mass-or-scale. A larger being's subjective second encompasses substantially more absolute time than a smaller being's subjective second. Symbolically: T_absolute_per_unit_subjective ∝ M.

The two formulations are mathematically equivalent — Formulation 2 is simply the reciprocal of Formulation 1 — but they emphasize different aspects of the framework's content. Formulation 1 emphasizes the rate at which subjective time flows; Formulation 2 emphasizes the absolute-time scope encompassed within subjective experience.

The Yahweh passage uses Formulation 2 explicitly: "when a millennium has gone by for you, the gigantic being of whom the sun is an atom has only had the time to take one step." Translated: a millennium of human absolute time corresponds to approximately one step of the gigantic being's subjective time — Formulation 2 in operational application.

The mathematical-status caveat

The Mass Effect framework has not been formally mathematized within the corpus. The principal articulation is qualitative — the inverse-proportionality between subjective-time-rate and mass-or-scale — without specific quantitative coefficients or specific functional forms.

Several specific mathematical questions remain open:

The functional form. Is the relationship strictly inverse-proportional (1/M), or some other functional form (1/M^α for some α, or some logarithmic function, or some other relationship)? The mainstream allometric scaling laws use specific powers (3/4 for metabolism, -1/4 for heart rate, 1/4 for lifespan), suggesting that the appropriate functional form for the Mass Effect may involve specific powers rather than strict inverse-proportionality.

The proportionality coefficient. What is the specific numerical coefficient relating subjective-time-rate to mass-or-scale? The Yahweh passage's thousand-years-per-step illustration provides one quantitative anchor, but the broader calibration across the full cosmological hierarchy is not specified.

The complexity-vs-mass distinction. How does the framework handle the distinction between mass and level of organization? The source's qualifying phrase suggests that pure kinematic mass is not the principal variable, but the broader complexity-or-organization variable is not formally specified.

The scale-boundary questions. Does the framework extend to all scales without limit, or are there specific scale ranges within which it operates? The infinite-hierarchy framework suggests the former, but the operational implications at extreme scales remain to be developed.

The corpus registers these open mathematical questions without claiming to resolve them. The Mass Effect framework's mature mathematization remains a future project, with the framework's qualitative content providing substantial predictive power across the empirical domains where the law has been tested.

The operational consequences

The Mass Effect framework has substantial operational consequences across multiple domains:

The non-perception of larger-scale beings. Humans cannot easily perceive the existence of larger-scale beings (the living Earth, the gigantic being of whom the sun is an atom) because the time-scale differences are too great. The Earth's "palpitations" occur on time scales (geological, planetary) that are too slow for human perception to register as the activity of a living being. The gigantic being's "step" takes a thousand human years and is thus imperceptible from the human perspective.

The non-perception of smaller-scale beings. Conversely, humans cannot easily perceive the existence of smaller-scale beings (the worlds within atoms, the beings on the cellular and subcellular scales) because the time-scale differences are too great in the other direction. The "millions of worlds being born and dying" within an atom of one's arm occur on time scales too rapid for human perception to register.

The non-communication across substantial scale differences. The source's specific consequence: "There cannot be any communication between an infinitely large entity and a universe of infinitely small entities, because the difference in mass is too great, thus creating a difference in the flow of equivalent time." Beings of substantially different scales cannot meaningfully communicate because the time-scaling differences prevent any practical information exchange. This has substantial theological-philosophical implications, treated below.

The theological-philosophical implications. The Mass Effect framework has substantial implications for the broader theological framework. If communication across infinite scale differences is impossible, then the conventional theological framework — in which a vastly-large deity communicates meaningfully with vastly-smaller human beings — becomes operationally impossible at the extreme scale differences traditionally posited. The Raëlian framework's specific theological position (the Elohim as creators of similar scale to humans rather than as infinitely-large divinities) is consistent with this Mass Effect-derived constraint, while the conventional supernatural-deity theological framework is not.

Connection to the Cosmic Chain framework

The Mass Effect framework has substantial structural connection to the broader Cosmic Chain framework (treated more fully in the Cosmic Chain entry).

The Cosmic Chain operates as the indefinitely extended sequence of created-and-creating civilizations across cosmic time. Each civilization in the chain operates at its own characteristic scale, with the prior creators (the Elohim, their predecessors, their predecessors' predecessors) and the future creators (humanity, humanity's future creations, their successors) operating within their own characteristic time scales. The Mass Effect framework provides the specific scaling relationship that governs the relationships between these various scale-distinguished civilizations.

The connection is operationally significant: the Cosmic Chain's broader cyclical-recursive structure operates within the Mass Effect framework's specific time-scaling content. The various creator civilizations across the chain are presumably of approximately similar scales to each other (as members of the broader humanoid-biological category), with the Mass Effect operating principally across the broader cosmological hierarchy rather than within the chain itself.

Connection to the Infinity framework

The Mass Effect framework operates within the broader Infinity framework that is the precondition for the framework's domain. The Infinity framework establishes:

  • Spatial infinity — the cosmos has no center and no boundary in any direction
  • Temporal infinity — the cosmos has no beginning and no end in temporal direction
  • Hierarchical infinity — intelligent civilizations exist at every scale from infinitely small to infinitely large

The Mass Effect operates principally on the hierarchical infinity dimension — the framework's specific content concerns how the experience of time scales across the infinite hierarchy of cosmological organization. Without infinite hierarchy, the Mass Effect would have only a finite domain to operate over (between specific minimum and maximum scales). With infinite hierarchy, the Mass Effect operates indefinitely across all scales, with the scaling relationship continuing without limit in both directions.

The detailed treatment of the Infinity framework lives in the Infinity entry when written; the Mass Effect entry's specific contribution is establishing the time-scaling relationship that operates within the broader infinite-hierarchical context.

Distinguishing from adjacent concepts

The Mass Effect vs. special-relativistic time dilation

Special-relativistic time dilation operates as a function of relative velocity rather than mass: t' = t·γ where γ depends on relative velocity rather than on the masses of the objects involved. The Mass Effect operates as a function of mass-or-scale rather than velocity.

The relationship is one of distinct-causal-relationships. Both phenomena involve time-dilation effects, but they operate through different causal mechanisms (velocity-based vs mass/scale-based). They can both operate simultaneously in specific physical situations.

The Mass Effect vs. general-relativistic gravitational time dilation

General-relativistic gravitational time dilation operates as a function of gravitational potential — clocks at lower gravitational potential run more slowly than clocks at higher gravitational potential. The Mass Effect framework has substantial structural overlap with gravitational time dilation but extends substantially beyond it.

The relationship is one of substantial-overlap-with-extension:

  • Overlap: Both treat mass as causing time-dilation effects
  • Extension: The Mass Effect extends gravitational time dilation through (a) the application to subjective-time and biological-cognitive scales rather than only to mainstream-physics-measurable absolute time, (b) the role of complexity-or-organization rather than purely gravitational-potential, and (c) the broader cosmological-hierarchical formulation across all scales

The Mass Effect framework can be read as a generalized version of gravitational time dilation that extends the relativistic framework to additional scales and additional aspects of time-experience that mainstream physics has not directly engaged.

The Mass Effect vs. biological time perception alone

Biological time perception scholarship (Eagleman, Wittmann, others) establishes that smaller animals perceive time at proportionally faster rates than larger animals. The Mass Effect framework includes this biological-time-perception content as a specific subset, while extending substantially beyond biological-perception scales to the broader cosmological-hierarchical scales.

The relationship is one of subset-relationship-with-broader-extension. Biological time perception is one specific empirical domain in which the Mass Effect framework's content has been substantially established; the broader Mass Effect framework extends to scales beyond the biological perception domain.

The Mass Effect vs. allometric scaling laws

The mainstream allometric scaling laws (Kleiber's law, heart-rate scaling, lifespan scaling) establish specific quantitative scaling relationships between body mass and various biological-temporal variables. The Mass Effect framework includes the broad allometric-scaling content as substantial empirical confirmation while extending beyond the biological scales.

The relationship is one of structural-empirical-consistency. The mainstream allometric scaling laws provide substantial empirical support for the broad type of relationship the Mass Effect framework articulates, while the framework's broader cosmological-hierarchical content goes substantially beyond what the biological scaling laws have established.

The Mass Effect vs. fractal cosmology generally

Fractal cosmology is the broader framework treating the cosmos as exhibiting self-similarity across scales. The Mass Effect is one specific dimension of the broader fractal-cosmological framework — the temporal dimension of the broader self-similarity.

The relationship is one of specific-dimension-within-broader-framework. Fractal cosmology is the broader framework concerning self-similar cosmological organization; the Mass Effect is the specific temporal-scaling dimension within that broader framework. The fractal-cosmological framework also includes spatial-self-similarity content (each scale containing within it the next scale down), structural-organizational content (each scale exhibiting similar organizational principles), and various other dimensions that the Mass Effect framework does not directly address.

The Mass Effect vs. the broader Infinity framework

The Infinity framework is the broader cosmological framework treating the cosmos as infinite in space, time, and hierarchical scales. The Mass Effect operates within the Infinity framework's hierarchical-infinite dimension — the framework's specific content concerns how time-scaling operates across the infinite hierarchy.

The relationship is one of specific-law-within-broader-framework. The Infinity framework is the broader cosmological precondition; the Mass Effect is one specific law operating within that broader precondition.

Modern reinterpretations

Mainstream relativity and gravitational time dilation

Mainstream relativistic physics has produced substantial work on time-dilation phenomena across the past century-plus.

Albert Einstein's special and general relativity (1905, 1915) established the principal modern framework for understanding time-dilation phenomena. The detailed treatment of Einstein's specific work lives in the broader physics-history scholarship; the Mass Effect entry's specific contribution is registering the relationship between mainstream relativity and the corpus's specific framework.

Empirical confirmations of gravitational time dilation include:

  • The Pound-Rebka experiment (Robert Pound and Glen Rebka Jr., 1959) at Harvard's Jefferson Physical Laboratory demonstrated gravitational redshift across a 22.5-meter vertical column with substantial precision
  • The Hafele-Keating experiment (Joseph Hafele and Richard Keating, 1971) demonstrated both special-relativistic and gravitational time dilation through atomic clocks flown on commercial aircraft
  • The Vessot-Levine experiment (Robert Vessot and Martin Levine, 1976) demonstrated gravitational time dilation through hydrogen maser clocks on the Gravity Probe A rocket, with precision substantially exceeding earlier tests
  • The 2010 NIST experiment (Chou, Hume, Rosenband, Wineland, National Institute of Standards and Technology) demonstrated gravitational time dilation across height differences as small as 33 centimeters
  • GPS satellite corrections continuously confirm the predicted time-dilation effects as a routine engineering necessity (the 38-microsecond-per-day clock-rate difference between GPS satellites and ground-based clocks)
  • Black hole observations (Event Horizon Telescope, gravitational-wave detection through LIGO/Virgo) have confirmed predictions about extreme gravitational-time-dilation environments

The integrated mainstream-relativistic position is that mass produces time-dilation effects through the specific mechanism of gravitational potential. Time runs more slowly in stronger gravitational fields, with the predicted effects empirically confirmed across substantial precision and across scales from sub-meter laboratory experiments to cosmological observations.

The framework's relationship to mainstream relativity is one of substantial-structural-consistency-with-substantial-extension. The framework accepts the relativistic content as substantially correct within its empirical domain, while extending the relativistic framework substantially through the broader cosmological-hierarchical formulation.

Biological time perception scholarship

Mainstream biological and neuroscientific scholarship on time perception has produced substantial work across the past several decades.

David Eagleman's research at Baylor College of Medicine and Stanford University has produced substantial findings on time perception. The principal works include The Brain: The Story of You (Pantheon, 2015), Livewired: The Inside Story of the Ever-Changing Brain (Pantheon, 2020), and various journal publications. Eagleman's specific findings include the empirical demonstration that smaller animals perceive time at proportionally faster rates, with substantial implications for the broader question of how time-experience scales with biological organization.

Marc Wittmann's research at the Institute for Frontier Areas of Psychology and Mental Health (Freiburg) has produced substantial work on human time perception, including Felt Time: The Psychology of How We Perceive Time (MIT Press, 2016) and various other publications. Wittmann's work focuses principally on subjective-time-experience variation in humans rather than across species, but provides substantial complementary content.

Critical flicker fusion frequency research has established that smaller and faster-moving animals can resolve temporal events at substantially higher frequencies than larger and slower animals. The principal findings include flies resolving flickering at 250+ Hz (where humans see steady light at 60 Hz), birds substantially exceeding human temporal resolution, and various other species-specific findings consistent with the Mass Effect framework's broader content.

The integrated mainstream-biological position is that time perception scales with body size and metabolic rate. The framework's relationship to mainstream biological time-perception scholarship is one of substantial-structural-consistency — the empirical findings substantially support the broad type of relationship the Mass Effect framework articulates within the biological-perceptual domain.

Allometric scaling laws

Mainstream biological scholarship on allometric scaling has produced substantial work across the past century.

Max Kleiber's metabolic scaling law (1932) established that metabolic rate scales as body mass to the power of 3/4: B ∝ M^(3/4). The relationship has been extensively verified across substantial portions of the animal kingdom.

The West-Brown-Enquist scaling theory (Geoffrey B. West, James H. Brown, Brian J. Enquist, 1997) provided the principal contemporary theoretical explanation for allometric scaling laws, deriving the 3/4 power from fractal-network theory regarding biological transport networks. The principal works include various Science and PNAS publications across the 1997-present period.

Geoffrey West's broader work, particularly Scale: The Universal Laws of Growth, Innovation, Sustainability, and the Pace of Life in Organisms, Cities, Economies, and Companies (Penguin Press, 2017), has extended the allometric scaling framework substantially beyond biology to cities, economies, and various other complex systems.

The integrated allometric-scaling position is that biological time scales systematically with body mass through specific power-law relationships. The framework's relationship to mainstream allometric-scaling scholarship is one of substantial-structural-consistency-with-broader-extension. The biological scaling laws provide specific quantitative content within the biological domain, while the Mass Effect framework's broader cosmological-hierarchical content extends beyond the biological scales.

Sendy on time

Jean Sendy developed substantial complementary content on time across his various works. The principal Sendy positions:

  • Time as parasitic to physics: Sendy treats time as "parasitic to all formulations and equations of physics," "an arbitrary element" without independent existence
  • The conventional-scientific-time critique: Sendy treats scientific time as "conventional, based on physical standards susceptible of being coordinated under different forms in arbitrary ways"
  • The relativistic-spacetime critique: Sendy treats relativistic theories that "confuse the dimensions of space and time" as "ridiculous," while acknowledging that the relativistic framework captures certain genuine empirical phenomena
  • The biological-mental origin of time: Sendy treats the notion of time as "subjective, derived from biological and mental organization, projected into the exterior world to create the irresistible illusion of an absolute universal time"

The framework's relationship to Sendy's specific position is one of substantial-overlap-with-specific-extension. The corpus accepts Sendy's broader position that subjective time is the principal reality and that absolute time is an abstraction, while developing the Mass Effect framework's specific content beyond what Sendy directly provides.

Biglino and Wallis on time

Mauro Biglino and Paul Anthony Wallis have produced limited specific engagement with the Mass Effect framework. Both scholars' broader work on the Hebrew Bible and the alliance-mediated history is structurally compatible with the Mass Effect framework, but neither has produced substantial specific engagement with the framework's distinctive content.

The framework's relationship to the broader landscape

The corpus's Mass Effect framework is positioned within this scholarly landscape as follows: substantially aligned with mainstream general-relativistic gravitational time dilation at the empirical level, while extending substantially through the broader cosmological-hierarchical formulation; substantially aligned with mainstream allometric scaling laws and biological time-perception scholarship at the biological level, while extending substantially through the broader cosmological-hierarchical formulation; substantially aligned with Sendy's broader critique of absolute time, while developing the specific Mass Effect content beyond what Sendy directly provides; substantially distinct from mainstream Newtonian conceptions of absolute universal time, which mainstream physics has itself substantially abandoned through the relativistic revolution.

Comparative observations

The Mass Effect framework's specific content has substantial parallels in various cultural-religious traditions worldwide.

Hindu yuga time-scaling

The Hindu tradition preserves the most extensively developed time-scaling cosmology in the world's religious traditions. The principal features:

The yuga durations. The four yugas have specific scaled durations: Krita Yuga (1,728,000 years), Treta Yuga (1,296,000 years), Dvapara Yuga (864,000 years), Kali Yuga (432,000 years), in the ratio 4:3:2:1.

The kalpa cosmology. One Mahayuga (the four yugas combined) totals 4,320,000 years; 1,000 mahayugas comprise one Kalpa of approximately 4.32 billion years; one Kalpa is described as one "day of Brahma," with each Kalpa followed by an equally long "night of Brahma."

The Brahma's lifespan. Brahma's lifespan totals 100 Brahma-years of 360 Brahma-days each, producing a total of approximately 311 trillion human years.

The structural scaling. The Hindu time-scale framework treats different cosmic scales as operating at substantially different time rates, with the broader pattern being that larger-scale beings (Brahma, the various devas) operate at substantially slower subjective-time rates than smaller-scale beings (humans, animals). The framework provides substantial structural parallel to the Mass Effect framework's specific content.

The detailed treatment of the Hindu yuga cosmology lives in the Cosmic Chain entry's Comparative observations; the Mass Effect entry's specific contribution is registering the time-scaling parallel to the framework's specific content.

Buddhist multi-temporal-realms doctrines

The Buddhist tradition preserves substantial material on multi-temporal-realms cosmology.

The deva-realms time-scaling. Various Buddhist texts specify that the deva-realms operate at substantially different time rates than the human realm. The specific framework: one day in the lowest deva-realm (Cātummahārājika) equals 50 human years; one day in the next deva-realm (Tāvatiṃsa) equals 100 human years; the scaling continues progressively through the higher deva-realms with substantially longer ratios.

The hell-realm time-scaling. Various Buddhist texts also specify that hell-realms operate at substantially different time rates than the human realm, with the specific pattern being that hell-realm subjective time is substantially longer than human time (one day in some hell-realms corresponding to substantial periods of human absolute time).

The Bodhisattva-path cosmic time scales. The Mahāyāna tradition's bodhisattva-path framework operates across substantial cosmic time scales, with various specific frameworks specifying the appropriate time scales for different stages of bodhisattva development.

The Buddhist multi-temporal-realms framework provides substantial structural parallel to the Mass Effect framework's specific content, with the various realms being read as operating at scale-appropriate subjective-time rates within the broader Buddhist cosmological framework.

Hermetic "as above, so below" and temporal correspondence

The Hermetic tradition's principle "as above, so below" (the Tabula Smaragdina / Emerald Tablet's specific phrasing) has substantial implications for temporal correspondence across cosmological scales. The principal features:

The cosmic correspondence principle. The Hermetic tradition treats the cosmic structure as operating through specific correspondences between the various levels of being — the macrocosm corresponding to the microcosm.

The temporal dimension. The "as above, so below" principle has substantial temporal implications: events at one cosmic scale correspond to analogous events at other scales, with the time scales being appropriately scaled to the size of each level. The principle provides substantial structural parallel to the Mass Effect framework's specific scaling content.

The Renaissance Hermetic development. The Renaissance Hermetic tradition (Ficino, Pico della Mirandola, Bruno, Dee) developed substantial content on cosmic-correspondence principles with various temporal-dimension implications.

The framework reads the Hermetic tradition as preserving substantial parallel content to the Mass Effect framework within the distinctive Hermetic philosophical-theological framing.

Indigenous mythological time-scales

Various indigenous traditions preserve substantial multi-temporal-scale cosmological content.

The Australian Aboriginal Dreamtime. The Aboriginal Australian Dreamtime (Tjukurpa, Jukurrpa, and various other regional terms) operates as a cosmic-temporal framework substantially distinct from ordinary linear time. The Dreamtime is the originary time during which the ancestral beings shaped the world; it is also a continuing dimension that operates alongside ordinary time, with substantial specific content on the relationship between the two temporal dimensions. The Aboriginal framework provides substantial parallel content to the Mass Effect framework's broader multi-temporal-scale content.

Mesoamerican calendar cycles. The Mesoamerican traditions (Aztec, Maya, Olmec) preserve substantial calendar-based time-scaling content. The Maya Long Count's 5,125-year cycle and the broader cosmic-temporal framework treat substantially different time scales as operating within the broader cosmological organization. The detailed treatment lives in the Cosmic Chain entry's Comparative observations.

Various other indigenous traditions preserve substantial parallel content within their distinctive cultural-religious framings, with the broader pattern being that indigenous traditions worldwide have preserved substantial awareness of multi-temporal-scale cosmological organization.

Greek philosophical time-treatments

The Greek philosophical tradition has produced substantial work on time across multiple distinct positions.

Plato's treatment of time in the Timaeus identifies time as "the moving image of eternity," with substantial implications for the broader relationship between temporal and eternal cosmic dimensions.

Aristotle's treatment of time in the Physics identifies time as "the number of motion with respect to before and after," with substantial implications for the relationship between time and motion-or-change.

The Stoic eternal recurrence doctrine treats cosmic time as cyclic, with successive cycles of conflagration and renewal operating across substantially long cosmic periods. The detailed treatment lives in the Cosmic Chain entry's Comparative observations.

Plotinus's treatment of time in the Enneads develops the Platonic framework substantially, with time being treated as a distinct ontological dimension related to but distinct from eternity.

The Greek philosophical tradition provides substantial philosophical-theoretical context within which the Mass Effect framework's specific content can be situated, even where the Greek tradition does not directly engage the specific cosmological-hierarchical scaling content.

The convergence

The corpus's working position on the comparative-time-scaling question is that the global recurrence of multi-temporal-scale cosmological frameworks across cultural-religious traditions is meaningful as evidence of a broader pattern. The mainstream scholarly explanation — which generally treats the various traditions as developing their own time-scaling content through internal religious-philosophical processes combined with cultural diffusion — is read by the corpus as substantially insufficient to account for the breadth and the specific structural parallels (the multi-temporal-scale content, the scale-appropriate time-rate frameworks, the systematic relationship between cosmic-hierarchy levels and characteristic time scales) that appear consistently across geographically and chronologically separated traditions.

The framework's specific reading is that the global multi-temporal-scale pattern preserves common memory of the broader cosmic-temporal situation that the Mass Effect framework articulates, with each cultural-religious tradition preserving the memory in its own terms and with its own specific details reflecting its own perspective and its own subsequent transmission history. The Hindu and Buddhist traditions preserve the memory most directly relative to the framework's specific scaling content; the Hermetic tradition preserves the cosmic-correspondence principle within Western philosophical-theological tradition; the indigenous and Greek philosophical traditions preserve substantial parallel content within their respective cultural channels.

The corpus does not require rejecting all of the mainstream scholarly framework. Cultural diffusion certainly occurred across the historical period, and the time-scaling traditions across cultures show both common structural features (preserving the original framework) and culturally specific elaborations (reflecting the diffusion and local development). What the corpus's framework adds is the underlying cosmic-temporal situation that gave rise to the structural commonalities — a situation that the mainstream framework, lacking the corpus's broader cosmological framework, has had no way to identify and has therefore had to attribute to combinations of independent religious-philosophical development and cultural diffusion.
