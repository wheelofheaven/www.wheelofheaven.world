Arche de Noé

L'arche de Noé est, dans la Genèse, le vaisseau permettant la survie des lignées vivantes pendant le Grand Déluge. Dans le corpus Wheel of Heaven, elle est lue comme une banque génétique opérationnelle conçue selon des spécifications élohimiques pour préserver la biodiversité terrestre.

Noah's Ark (Hebrew תֵּבַת נֹחַ tevat Noaḥ, "container/sealed-vessel of Noah") is, in the Wheel of Heaven framework, the orbital spacecraft constructed under the technical direction of the exiled-creator faction (the Serpentine faction, treated in the Satan entry) and crewed by Noah and his family, designed to preserve the Eden-lineage human family and the genetic material of Earth's biosphere through the Council-ordered nuclear-missile cataclysm at the Cancer-Gemini boundary (approximately 6,690 BCE in the corpus chronology). The principal source articulation appears in Claude Vorilhon's The Book Which Tells the Truth (1974), in "The Truth" chapter, "The Flood" section:

"When the exiled creators were informed of the project they asked Noah to build a spaceship, which would orbit the Earth during the cataclysm containing a pair of each species that was to be preserved.

This was true figuratively speaking, but in reality — and your scientific knowledge will very soon enable you to understand this — a single living cell of each species, male and female, is all that is required to recreate a whole being. This is something like the first living cell of a fetus in the womb of its mother, which already possesses all the information needed to create a human being right down to the color of its eyes and hair. This was a colossal task, but it was completed on time.

When the explosion took place, life had already been preserved a few thousand kilometers above the Earth. The continent was submerged by a gigantic tidal wave, which destroyed all forms of life on its surface."

The corpus reads the Hebrew תֵּבָה (tevah) as designating a sealed orbital craft rather than a wooden boat. The reading is supported by three textual considerations. First, the tevah word itself: the Hebrew designates a sealed vessel or closed container, distinct from the standard Hebrew word for ship (oniyyah, אֳנִיָּה), which appears throughout the Hebrew Bible for naval craft but does not appear in Genesis 6-9. The only other Hebrew Bible usage of tevah is Exodus 2:3-5, describing the basket in which baby Moses was placed in the Nile — registering parallel sealed-vessel content. Second, the Genesis 6:16 three-story specification ("with lower, second, and third stories shalt thou make it") registers multi-deck interior arrangement consistent with spacecraft engineering. Third, the Genesis 7:17 phrase vatarom me-al ha-aretz (וַתָּרָם מֵעַל הָאָרֶץ, "was lifted up above the earth") uses the vertical-directional preposition מֵעַל (me-al, "from over, above"), preserving directional content that conventional translations have often obscured by rendering as "above the earth" in the sense of "above the ground level." The Vorilhon source emphasizes this reading: "As you can clearly see, it is said that the ark was lifted 'above' the Earth and not 'on' the water."

The ark's actual cargo was genetic. The Vorilhon source registers the specific biological claim — that a single living cell contains sufficient information to regenerate the complete organism — that has moved from speculation to established fact in the decades since the 1974 articulation. The 1996 cloning of Dolly the sheep demonstrated publicly that a complete mammal could be regenerated from a single somatic cell of an adult donor. The Frozen Zoo at the San Diego Zoo Wildlife Alliance has been collecting cell samples from endangered species since 1972, with recent cloning of black-footed ferrets and Przewalski's horses demonstrating practical viability at small scale. The Svalbard Global Seed Vault (opened 2008) provides analogous infrastructure for plant species, with over a million seed samples preserved against catastrophic loss. The contemporary scientific trajectory toward what the corpus framework presupposes for the ark operation is visible in component form, though the integration at planetary scale that the ark required is not yet operational in contemporary engineering.

The ark's human crew was eight: Noah, his wife, his three sons (Shem, Ham, and Japheth), and their wives. The corpus reads the eight-crew figure as encoded in the age's association with the eighth day — the cosmic-symbolic content that the broader biblical numerical engagement (substantive 1 Peter 3:20's reference to "eight souls saved by water") preserves. The Vorilhon source registers additional content: the ark, when it returned to the surface, "carried a couple from each race of human beings on the Earth" — suggesting that representatives of the seven human lineages, not only the Eden lineage from which Noah came, were preserved and returned. Whether these other representatives were living humans on board or were regenerated from preserved genetic material after landing is not specified precisely by the source.

The construction of the ark was, on the corpus reading, an open act of civil disobedience conducted across centuries on Earth's surface under continuous Council observation. The timeline.epub engagement:

"It is worth registering what the construction of the ark represented politically. The Serpentine faction, in undertaking it, was committing an act of open resistance against the Council's decision. The Council had ordered the destruction of all life on Earth. The exiled creators, by building the ark and preserving the genetic material of the biosphere they had spent ten thousand years constructing, were countermanding the Council's order. They could not have done this in secret — the ark was an enormous engineering project, requiring centuries to complete, conducted on the surface of a planet under continuous observation from the home world. The Council knew. The exiled creators were defying it openly."

The Council's response during the construction period was — significantly — not to attack the project. The Council watched. The Council allowed the construction to proceed. The corpus reading: the Council calculated that the preserved remnant would not be a strategic threat. This calculation would prove wrong — the post-flood recovery would produce, within centuries, a renewed civilization capable of constructing the Tower of Babel — but at the time of construction, Council tolerance reflected an assessment that the preserved remnant would be manageable through subsequent negotiation rather than requiring active military suppression.

Sommaire

    Wheel of Heaven est une lecture ouverte et multilingue des traditions de création du monde antique — raëliennes, bibliques, mésopotamiennes et autres — à travers une seule hypothèse de travail : que les êtres appelés Élohim étaient une petite civilisation humaine avancée venue d'ailleurs, qui est arrivée ici, a conçu la vie sur Terre et a laissé un récit dans les textes religieux. La lecture se déploie sur douze âges du cycle de la précession, depuis le début du projet il y a vingt-deux mille ans jusqu'à l'âge actuel de la révélation et dans le long avenir qu'ouvre la prochaine Grande Année. Le site est libre, attentif à ses sources, et conçu pour que le travail puisse être vérifié.
