The swastika is one of the oldest religious-iconographic symbols globally, with archaeological evidence of usage extending back at least 12,000 years to Paleolithic Europe. The symbol consists of a cross with four arms bent at right angles, typically rotated 90 degrees between successive arms, with the principal orientations being right-facing (clockwise, svastika proper in Hindu tradition) and left-facing (counter-clockwise, sauvastika). The symbol exhibits substantial cross-cultural distribution across virtually all major cultural-religious traditions: it is one of the principal auspicious symbols in Hindu tradition (where the Sanskrit etymology svastika means "well-being" or "good fortune"); appears in nearly all Buddhist temples globally, with substantial usage on the Buddhapada (Buddha's footprint) and in the Chinese 卍 (manji) form; is one of the eight auspicious symbols (ashtamangala) in Jain tradition; and has substantial pre-Nazi usage across European, Mesopotamian, Mediterranean, Native American, and various other cultural-religious traditions. The symbol's specific semantic content varies across traditions but consistently involves auspicious, protective, and cosmic-cyclic content.

Etymology and naming

The symbol has substantial cross-cultural designations operating across multiple traditions and historical contexts.

Sanskrit "svastika" as principal etymological anchor

The Sanskrit term svastika (सुस्तिक, also transliterated as svastika, swastika, or suastika) is the principal etymological anchor for the symbol's contemporary international designation. The construction derives from:

  • sv- (सु-): a Sanskrit prefix meaning "good," "well," or "auspicious"
  • asti (अस्ति): the third-person-singular present-tense form of the Sanskrit verb as- (to be), meaning "is" or "exists"
  • *ka (-क): a Sanskrit suffix forming nominal substantives

The composite construction sv-asti-ka therefore translates approximately as "that which is good" or "the well-being-thing" or "the auspicious." The Sanskrit etymology has been the principal etymological anchor across mainstream international scholarship since the late nineteenth century, with the term svastika being adopted into French, English, German, and various other European-language scholarly contexts during this period.

  • Latin: crux gammata — the Latin scholarly designation, again referring to the four-gamma composition

The principal subsequent contributions include various twentieth-century archaeological-cultural-anthropological works (Joseph Campbell's broader comparative-mythology work; Marija Gimbutas's work on Neolithic European symbolism; various other contributors), continuing extensive archaeological documentation of the symbol's specific historical-cultural distribution.

ラエル運動の無限のシンボルは、ダビデの星と卍を組み合わせたものであり、その結合はエロヒム的記憶に根ざした無限の循環を凝縮する。Wheel of Heaven は、両記号の比較史を背景にその記号論を論じる。

概要
type
Religious-iconographic symbol; one of the oldest known symbols globally with cross-cultural distribution across virtually all major cultural-religious traditions
basic_form
Cross with four arms bent at right angles, typically rotated 90 degrees between successive arms; principal orientations are right-facing (clockwise, svastika proper) and left-facing (counter-clockwise, sauvastika)
sanskrit_etymology
Svastika (सुस्तिक) from sv- (good, well) and asti (to be) plus the -ka suffix; principal meaning 'well-being,' 'good fortune,' 'auspiciousness'
oldest_known_usage
Approximately 10,000 BCE; Mezin culture (Ukraine), carved-mammoth-ivory bracelet
principal_traditional_meanings
Auspiciousness, good fortune, well-being (Hindu); cyclic cosmic time (Buddhist, Jain); eternity, infinity in time (Raëlian framework); the cycle, the choice between progress and regression (Raëlian-specific framework content)
principal_cross_cultural_traditions
Hindu (svastika), Buddhist (Buddhapada, manji 卍), Jain (ashtamangala), Paleolithic and Neolithic European (Mezin, Cucuteni-Trypillian, Vinča), Bronze Age and Iron Age European (Greek geometric pottery, Etruscan, Roman, Celtic-Germanic-Slavic-Baltic), Mesopotamian, ancient Mediterranean, Native American (Navajo, Hopi), various indigenous traditions
position_in_raelian_framework
Central element of the original Raëlian Symbol of Infinity (1973-present, in use in Asian Raëlian movement branches); modified to galaxy-spiral in Western Raëlian movement branches (1990 modification); represents the temporal dimension of infinity through the cyclic-eternal principle
post_1945_situation
Nazi appropriation (1920-1945) produced substantial cultural-political complications in post-Holocaust European-North American contexts; legal restrictions vary across jurisdictions (Germany StGB §86 prohibits Nazi-specific usage; various other European countries have similar restrictions); various post-1945 reclamation efforts within Hindu-Buddhist diaspora communities have worked toward distinguishing the broader pre-Nazi religious-traditional usage from the specific Nazi appropriation
distinguished_from
The Star of David (the other principal component of the Raëlian Symbol of Infinity, treated under separate entry); the Raëlian Symbol of Infinity as composite emblem (treated under separate entry); the Nazi Hakenkreuz specifically (the tilted-clockwise form adopted as NSDAP symbol 1920); the broader cross-cultural sun-cross category (which includes various non-swastika cross-with-arms forms)
status_in_source_material
Substantially documented; the principal Yahweh-delivered passages establish both the symbol's specific Raëlian-framework semantic content and its identification with broader cross-cultural traditional usage
principal_text
The Book Which Tells the Truth (1974), 'Watching Over the Chosen People' content (the principal Yahweh passage on the emblem); Extra-Terrestrials Took Me to Their Planet (1975), 'Buddhism' / 'Devils' section (the swastika as cycle); Extra-Terrestrials Took Me to Their Planet (1975), 'Neither God nor Soul' section (the Bardo Thodol cross-reference)

Wheel of Heaven は、古代世界の創造伝承——ラエル派、聖書、メソポタミア、その他——を、ただひとつの作業仮説を通して読む、開かれた多言語の読解です。すなわち、エロヒムと呼ばれる存在は、よそから来た小さな先進的な人間の文明であり、ここに到来し、地球上の生命を設計し、宗教的諸文献にその記録を残した、という仮説です。この読解は歳差周期の十二の時代にわたって広がります。二万二千年前のプロジェクトの始まりから、現在の開示の時代を経て、次の大いなる年が開く長い未来へと続きます。本サイトはオープンで、典拠を明示し、作業を検証できるよう構築されています。
