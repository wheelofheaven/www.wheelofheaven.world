エロヒムの母星

エロヒムの母星 — Wheel of Heaven の正典項目については「エロヒム母星」を参照。

The Elohim Home Planet is, in the Wheel of Heaven framework, the extrasolar world from which the Elohim — the alliance of advanced extraterrestrial scientists who, in the corpus reading, conducted the genetic-engineering project that produced terrestrial life — originate and to which their political-civilizational center remains attached. The principal source content appears in Claude Vorilhon's Message from the Designers (Tagman Press, 2005), consolidating the three principal Vorilhon contact-record books: Le Livre qui dit la vérité (1974), Les Extra-Terrestres m'ont emmené sur leur planète (1975), and Accueillir les Extra-Terrestres (1979).

The home planet's specifications, as preserved in the source:

  • Distance from its star: 236,000 parasangs (~70.8 billion kilometers), substantially further than Earth's distance from the Sun, because the home star is substantially larger than the Sun and therefore hotter at any given orbital distance
  • Distance from Earth: 30,000,000 parasangs (~9 trillion kilometers, approximately one light-year), with the substantial astronomical inconsistency the source itself addresses
  • Environment: mild temperature, lush vegetation, exotic flora, azure seas — the source description has the character of a paradisal landscape
  • Civilization: approximately seven billion inhabitants, organized into seven provinces
  • Language: official language closely resembling ancient Hebrew
  • Government: a Council of the Eternals of approximately 700 Elohim members presided by Yahweh (the first individual on whom the cloning-and-memory-transfer immortality technology was successfully applied, approximately 25,000 years ago), with approximately 8,400 resurrected humans from Earth also living on the planet
  • Economy: post-scarcity, organized around atomic-based energy and biological-robot labor, with intellectual-creative activity as the principal human pursuit
  • Travel technology: atom-based propulsion at seven times the speed of light, permitting the journey from the home planet to Earth in approximately two months at the time of Vorilhon's 1973-1975 contact, with subsequent technological advance to substantially shorter journey times by 1975

The parasang is a unit the source has redefined for its specific purpose. Etymologically the term derives from an ancient Persian linear-distance unit (used in Greek and Near Eastern texts for roughly three to four miles on a caravan road); the source has appropriated the word and redefined it as a unit of celestial distance equal to the distance light travels in one second (~300,000 kilometers), in the same way modern astronomy has appropriated the word parsec for a unit wholly unrelated to its etymological origins.

The source content allows two readings of the home planet's relationship to what the corpus calls "the Planet of the Eternals":

  • Single-planet reading: The home planet is the Planet of the Eternals. Yahweh's statement in Message from the Designers — "This residence is, in fact, very large, since it is an entire planet where the members of the Council of the Eternals live as well" — reads most naturally as identifying the residence (where Vorilhon was taken in 1975) with the entire home planet.
  • Two-planet reading: The Planet of the Eternals is a second smaller planet in the same stellar system as the main home planet. The corpus timeline.epub engagement articulates this reading, treating the Council of the Eternals' residence as distinct from the broader home-civilization population.

The Wheel of Heaven framework registers both readings as compatible with the source material. The pending Planet of the Eternals entry will articulate the specific Council-of-Eternals residence content; this entry articulates the broader home-planet content.

The home planet's location has been variously identified in adjacent traditions. The Mormon Book of Abraham registers a celestial body called Kolob described as "the heavenly body nearest to the throne of God." John Tvedtnes's 2005 essay "Authentic Ancient Names and Words in the Book of Abraham and Related Kirtland Egyptian Papers" proposes that Kolob etymologically derives from the Semitic root KLB ("dog"), suggesting that Kolob refers to Sirius, the "dog star" — the brightest star in the night sky and the principal star of the constellation Canis Major. The Kolob-Sirius hypothesis is one Mormon-internal interpretation among several; the Vorilhon source itself does not identify the home planet with Sirius or with any specific known star system. The Hebrew Bible content the source engages includes Psalm 19:4's "In them hath he set a tabernacle for the sun" and the Kabbalistic "height of the creator" tradition from which the parasang measurements derive. The broader cross-cultural pattern of "celestial home of the gods" traditions appears across virtually every major world religion (treated systematically in the Comparative observations section below).

The corpus position: the specific identification of the home planet with any contemporary known star system remains genuinely open. The Vorilhon source's deliberate obfuscation and the substantial astronomical-inconsistency in the stated distance both indicate that precise identification was not the source's intention. The Mormon Kolob-Sirius hypothesis is adjacent interpretive engagement worth registering while distinct from anything the Vorilhon source itself claims.

This entry articulates the Elohim Home Planet as a place — its location and astronomical specifications, its environment and civilization, its political and economic organization, its travel technology and connection to Earth, the relationship to the Planet of the Eternals, the Hebrew Bible and Kabbalistic source-textual engagement, the Mormon Kolob tradition with the Tvedtnes-Sirius hypothesis, the contemporary scientific context of exoplanet discovery, and the broader cross-cultural celestial-home traditions.

Location and astronomical specifications

The source's specification of the home planet's location operates through the parasang measurement system, with two principal distances given.

The parasang unit

The source's use of parasang deserves careful articulation. Etymologically the term derives from Old Persian parasanga (preserved in Greek as παρασάγγης, parasangēs, in Herodotus and other classical sources), designating a linear distance on caravan-route travel equivalent to roughly three to four miles or about one hour's march. The unit was widely used across the Achaemenid Empire and subsequent Persian and Near Eastern administrative contexts.

The Vorilhon source appropriates the term and redefines it as a unit of celestial distance equal to the distance light travels in one second, approximately 300,000 kilometers. The redefinition operates analogously to modern astronomy's appropriation of the word parsec (parallax-second), whose etymological origin in the parallax measurement method has long since become invisible to ordinary usage.

The source's reasoning for the redefinition is preserved in Message from the Designers:

"It is written that the 'height of the creator' is 236,000 'parasangs', and that 'the height of his heels' is 30,000,000 'parasangs'. The parasang is a unit of measurement just like the parsec, which stands for the distance that light can travel in one second, which is about 300,000 kilometers."

The "It is written" attribution points to the Kabbalistic tradition — specifically the Shi'ur Qomah literature, the early-medieval Jewish mystical tradition that articulates the "measure of the body" of the divine through specific numerical specifications including the figures the source quotes. The Vorilhon source operates on this tradition as preserving authentic content about the home planet's astronomical situation, with the "height of the creator" reading as the home planet's orbital distance from its star, and the "height of his heels" reading as the distance from Earth.

Distance from the home star

The home planet orbits its star at a distance of 236,000 parasangs = ~70.8 billion kilometers = ~474 AU (astronomical units, where 1 AU = the Earth-Sun distance). For reference, this is substantially beyond the orbit of Neptune (~30 AU) and well into what would in our solar system be the inner Oort cloud or trans-Neptunian region.

The substantial distance does not place the home planet in a frozen orbit because the home star is substantially larger than the Sun. The source states the home star is "a very big star" without specifying its precise mass, spectral class, or luminosity. A star substantially more luminous than the Sun would have its habitable zone (the orbital range where liquid water can exist on a planetary surface) pushed substantially outward — possibly to the substantial distance the source specifies.

The astronomical implications of this specification:

  • The home star is likely a substantially massive main-sequence star or a substantially evolved subgiant/giant. A main-sequence star with a habitable zone extending to ~474 AU would need to be substantially more luminous than the Sun — by approximately the square of the distance ratio, ~225,000 times solar luminosity (since habitable-zone distance scales as the square root of luminosity). Such a star would be a substantially massive O-type or early B-type star with a substantially short main-sequence lifetime (millions to tens of millions of years), which would not permit the substantial civilizational development the source describes.
  • The alternative is that the source's "habitable zone" extends substantially differently from how mainstream astronomy currently defines it. The variable-lightspeed cosmology the source proposes (treated below) may imply substantially different habitability criteria.
  • Or the source's specification is approximate rather than precise. The 236,000 parasangs figure may reflect a substantively true characteristic (distance substantially greater than Earth-Sun) without precision in the specific orbital mechanics.

The corpus does not require resolution of these astronomical questions. The substantive content is that the home planet orbits a substantially larger star at a substantially greater distance than Earth orbits the Sun, with the specific orbital dynamics calibrated for habitability by beings comparable to humans.

Distance from Earth

The home planet lies 30,000,000 parasangs = ~9 trillion kilometers = approximately 0.95 light-years from Earth. The figure is "just a little less than a light year" in the source's own phrasing.

The astronomical inconsistency this figure produces is substantial and the source addresses it directly. The nearest known star system to the Sun is the Alpha Centauri system (which includes Proxima Centauri), at approximately 4.24 light-years. No star is known to lie within 1 light-year of the Sun. The source's stated distance therefore conflicts with the contemporary astronomical observation that the corpus reader is presumed to have.

The source offers two responses to this objection:

Response 1: Deliberate obfuscation. The Elohim do not want the precise location of their planet to be known. The 1-light-year figure is therefore stated as a lower bound rather than as a precise measurement, with the actual distance substantially greater. As the source articulates this:

"The Elohim do not want us to know exactly where their planet is located. This is quite understandable when we consider man's persistence towards destruction, even though the level of technology on Earth is still fairly primitive."

The deliberate-obfuscation reading is internally consistent with the source's broader commitment to caution about disclosing information that could be used for harm before humanity has demonstrated readiness to receive it.

Response 2: Variable-lightspeed cosmology. The source proposes that the speed of light is not a universal constant but varies across regions of space, depending on the local density of the substance the source calls a "sub-quantum medium" pervading all space. On this view, the standard light-year-as-distance assumption (light travels at ~300,000 km/s, therefore 1 light-year = ~9 trillion km) holds only in regions where the local lightspeed is what we measure it to be near Earth. In other regions, light travels at different speeds, and the apparent distance to a star calculated from light-travel time is therefore inaccurate.

The source's articulation of this position in Message from the Designers:

"I might add that light does not move at the same speed in all strata of the universe, because nothing is invariable, neither in space nor time. This is one of the biggest errors that present scientists are committing. They start with an observation based on a limited period of time to draw conclusions bearing on the past thousands of years and on the time to come, or they base conclusions on a limited space, to draw conclusions on the infinity of space. Man has always made the same mistake of judging according to his knowledge."

A Raëlian-movement-internal hypothesis articulated in the source as additional explanation: the distance between the home planet and Earth could be approximately four light-years "by following the light" (which moves in a "pronounced curve") while being only one light-year by straight-line travel. The hypothesis is registered as a possibility in the source without endorsement.

The corpus engages this question carefully. The variable-lightspeed proposal is not as scientifically untenable as it may initially appear — the contemporary cosmological literature contains substantive engagement with variable-speed-of-light (VSL) cosmologies (João Magueijo's substantial work, others). The corpus does not need to resolve the question to articulate the source content; what matters is that the distance is substantially given as approximately one light-year, with the substantial astronomical inconsistency acknowledged and addressed by the source itself.

The contemporary exoplanet-discovery context

The contemporary scientific context for the source's home-planet specification has substantially developed since Vorilhon's 1973-1975 contact. The principal developments:

  • The exoplanet revolution. The first confirmed exoplanets were detected in the 1990s (51 Pegasi b in 1995 as the first around a main-sequence star). The contemporary catalog contains more than 5,000 confirmed exoplanets with substantial subsequent discoveries through the Kepler, TESS, and JWST programs.
  • Habitable-zone candidates. Several hundred confirmed exoplanets lie within their host stars' habitable zones, where liquid water could exist on a planetary surface. The principal nearby habitable-zone candidates include Proxima Centauri b (4.24 light-years, the nearest exoplanet to Earth), the TRAPPIST-1 system (39.6 light-years, with seven Earth-sized planets and several within the habitable zone), and substantial other systems.
  • No habitable-zone planet within 1 light-year of the Sun. The contemporary observational evidence does not support the existence of any planetary system within 1 light-year of the Sun. The nearest stars (Proxima Centauri, Alpha Centauri A and B) are at 4.24 light-years; no closer stellar object has been detected.

The contemporary scientific context therefore substantially confirms the astronomical inconsistency the Vorilhon source itself acknowledges. The source's responses (deliberate obfuscation; variable-lightspeed cosmology) remain the substantive engagement with this inconsistency.

Environment and ecology

The source's description of the home planet's environment derives principally from Vorilhon's 1975 visit, recorded in the second principal source-book (Les Extra-Terrestres m'ont emmené sur leur planète).

The paradisal landscape

Vorilhon's account of his arrival on the home planet:

"The temperature was very mild, and the air was perfumed with countless scents of exotic flowers. We walked towards the top of a hill, and a marvelous panorama began to appear. Innumerable small streams wound through the lush vegetation, and far off an azure sea sparkled in the sun."

The principal features registered:

  • Mild climate — temperature comfortable for unprotected human exposure; no extreme weather noted in the source description
  • Rich olfactory environment — substantial atmospheric content of plant-derived aromatic compounds, suggesting substantial biodiversity in floral species
  • Substantial fresh-water hydrological systems — the "innumerable small streams winding through the lush vegetation" suggesting a substantial freshwater cycle
  • Substantial vegetation cover — the "lush vegetation" suggesting Earth-comparable or substantially richer biomass productivity
  • Open marine environments — the "azure sea sparkled in the sun" indicating substantial open-water surfaces
  • Topographic variation — hills and panoramic views suggesting substantial geological differentiation

The cumulative description has the character of an Earth-comparable habitable planet — possibly substantially more biologically productive than Earth, possibly with substantial environmental management by the host civilization.

The agricultural and dining environment

The dining scene Vorilhon describes provides additional environmental content:

"Some people came out of a small cave located right next to us and approached us carrying trays piled high with fruits, grilled meats accompanied by the most incredible sauces, and drinks of unforgettable flavors."

The substantial fruit availability and the diverse culinary preparations indicate substantial agricultural productivity. The source elsewhere indicates that food production on the home planet is substantially automated through biological robots, with the eternals (both Elohim and resurrected humans) consuming the produced food without participating in its production.

The architectural and built environment

The source's description of the eternals' built environment:

"We can live in different cities with multiple architectural styles in greatly varied sites that we can modify at will. People fulfill themselves as they wish, only doing what they like to do."

The architectural variation suggests substantial cultural-aesthetic diversity within the home civilization. The "modify at will" specification indicates substantial environmental-engineering capacity — the eternals can substantially reshape their immediate environment as they prefer, presumably through the biological-robot infrastructure that performs the actual construction work.

Civilization and population

The home planet's civilization is the political-civilizational center of the alliance that conducted the Earth project. The source content articulates several substantive features.

Population

The total home-planet population is approximately seven billion, comparable to contemporary Earth's population (~8 billion as of the corpus compilation). The number is substantial enough to support a complex industrial-technological civilization while not so large as to substantially exceed the planet's carrying capacity.

The population control mechanism: each couple is permitted only two children, maintaining population stability at replacement rate. The eternals (members of the Council of the Eternals, both Elohim and resurrected humans) are not permitted to have children — a reproductive sterilization is performed on those who achieve eternal status, reversible if eternal status is subsequently relinquished. The mechanism prevents substantial population growth from indefinite-lifespan individuals while preserving the broader civilization's reproductive continuity.

The seven provinces

The home planet is organized into seven provinces, each with substantial political representation. The seven-province structure parallels the seven Eloha-team structure of the Earth creation project (treated in the Plurality of Gods and adjacent entries) — though the source does not articulate whether the seven home-planet provinces correspond directly to the seven Eloha teams or represent a separate seven-fold organization.

The provinces have rotating representatives on the intergalactic ship operating as the alliance's Earth-proximate operational base:

"On our planet there are seven provinces. Each has a representative on that vessel. If we add the two who are responsible for the vessel, there are permanently nine of us."

The seven provincial representatives plus two ship-responsible Elohim equals nine permanent personnel on the operational base.

Language

The home planet's official language closely resembles ancient Hebrew:

"Our official language closely resembles ancient Hebrew."

The substantive content of this claim: the Hebrew language preserved in the Hebrew Bible and subsequent Jewish tradition is, on the corpus reading, substantially derived from the home-civilization language transmitted to the Israel-team humans during the Earth project. The relationship is not coincidental — Hebrew preserves substantive linguistic content from the original alliance transmission, with the various subsequent Hebrew-language developments operating as natural-language drift from the original substrate.

The relationship has substantive implications for the corpus's broader engagement with Hebrew Bible language. The Hebrew terms the corpus engages directly — Elohim, Yahweh, ruach, kavod, nachash, bnei ha-elohim, nephilim, and others — preserve substantive content from the alliance transmission rather than being merely Iron Age Levantine theological vocabulary.

The Council of the Eternals

The Council of the Eternals is the principal governing body of the home civilization. The Council consists of:

  • Approximately 700 Elohim members. These are the Elohim who have qualified for eternal status — Yahweh and the senior alliance officers who have undergone the cloning-and-memory-transfer immortality process and who function as the political authority of the civilization.
  • Approximately 8,400 resurrected humans from Earth. These are humans who during their lives "reached a sufficient level of open-mindedness towards the infinite, or who enabled humanity on Earth to progress from its primitive level through their discoveries, their writings, their ways of organizing society and their exemplary acts of fraternity, love or selflessness." The resurrected humans live alongside the Elohim members of the Council.

The total Council population is approximately 9,100 eternals (700 Elohim + 8,400 resurrected humans). The source elsewhere uses the approximate figure of "slightly more than 9,000" eternals.

The Council is presided by Yahweh, the senior member of the relocating expedition that conducted the Earth project and the figure whose presidency of the Council extends across the entire subsequent operation. Yahweh's specific status: the first individual on whom the cloning-and-memory-transfer immortality technology was successfully applied, approximately 25,000 years ago. His seniority, his presidency of the Council, and his specific operational role across the entire alliance-Earth narrative all derive from his having been the first beneficiary of the technology.

The detailed treatment of the Council of the Eternals lives in the Council of the Eternals entry; the detailed treatment of Yahweh lives in the Yahweh entry.

Economy

The home civilization operates as a post-scarcity economy organized around several principal features:

  • Atomic-based energy. The home civilization's principal energy source is "highly developed atomic energy" — substantially more advanced than contemporary Earth nuclear technology, including a "closed-circuit" atomic application and substantial solar-energy use. The source specifies that the home civilization does not necessarily use uranium in its atomic reactors but several other "simple and harmless materials" — possibly indicating substantial advances in fusion or other non-fission atomic technologies.
  • Biological robots. All mechanical and service labor is performed by biological robots — engineered organisms produced through the same scientific principles used to create the people of Earth, but substantially limited and absolutely submissive. The biological robots have no aspirations, no pleasures except those necessary for their specialization, and no capacity for violence. Each eternal has approximately ten biological robots performing dirty and uninteresting work, for a total robot population of approximately 90,000 on the eternal-residence subsection of the planet.
  • No money or property. The post-scarcity economy operates without money. Property does not exist in any conventional sense — "everyone can have everything." The substantial productive capacity (biological-robot labor, atomic energy, automated manufacturing) meets all consumption needs without requiring market-based allocation.
  • Intellectual-creative activity as principal pursuit. Most home-civilization work is intellectual:

"Most of our work is intellectual, as our level of scientific development allows us to use robots for everything. We work only when we feel the inclination — and then only with our brain. Only our artists and our sports people work with their bodies and only because they have chosen this."

The post-scarcity-economy model on the home planet operates as the substantive precedent for the Paradism transition the corpus articulates for Earth's Aquarian-age development (treated in the Paradism entry). The corpus's vision of Earth's Golden Age is, in substantial part, the application of the home-civilization economic model to Earth conditions.

Social organization

The home civilization's social organization includes several distinctive features:

  • No marriage as institution. Couples exist where individuals choose them, but freedom is preserved: "men and women are both free. Couples exist. Those who have chosen to live as such may do so, but they may have their freedom back whenever they wish."
  • No jealousy. The source specifies that jealousy does not exist among the eternals: "we all love one another. Jealousy does not exist, since everyone can have everything, and property is nonexistent."
  • Sensual fulfillment. Sexual and broader sensual activity is substantially central to home-civilization life: "we always do things we enjoy doing — especially making love. We find our women very beautiful, and we make the most of this."
  • Biological-robot companions. Eternals may have biological-robot companions of either sex with custom physical specifications, including replicas of admired persons from Earth. The robot companions are absolutely submissive and incapable of suffering or jealousy.

The social organization on the home planet has substantial implications for the corpus's broader engagement with sexuality, relationship structure, and human flourishing — themes the corpus engages across multiple entries.

Lifespan and reproduction

The home-civilization population's natural lifespan is approximately 700 years, with the help of "a small surgical adjustment" the source identifies as the biblical "tree of life":

"We are not eternal, but we are able to live ten times longer than you, thanks to a small surgical adjustment, which in effect is the biblical 'tree of life.'"

The standard 700-year lifespan operates for the general home-civilization population. The Council of the Eternals members live indefinitely through the cloning-and-memory-transfer process — a substantially more advanced technology than the tree-of-life surgical intervention, available only to those who qualify for eternal status.

The reproduction restrictions:

  • General population: two children per couple, maintaining population stability
  • Eternals: no reproduction, with a reversible sterilization procedure

The reproductive restriction on eternals exists to prevent population overflow from indefinite-lifespan individuals. The substantial reproductive instincts of the eternals are, the source suggests, channeled into other-planet creation projects — the eternals who lose their reproductive role on the home planet substantially redirect their procreative drive into the creation of life on other planets, including the Earth project itself.

Travel technology and connection to Earth

The substantial distance from the home planet to Earth (approximately 1 light-year per the source specification) requires substantial travel technology. The source articulates the specific propulsion method and the broader operational infrastructure.

Atom-based propulsion at superluminal speeds

The source specifies that the Elohim craft do not travel by thermochemical rocketry (the method on which contemporary human space travel depends). Rocketry would require approximately 26,000 years for the journey from the home planet to Earth at contemporary rocket speeds — substantially too slow for operational use.

The actual propulsion method is described as "atom-based" and permits travel at seven times the speed of light:

"We have the means to travel to Earth from our planet in less than two months with an atom-based propulsion method, which enables us to move at the speed of rays that are seven times faster than the speed of light. Those rays 'carry' us. To be carried by them, we leave the optical window, which is the spectrum of rays detected by the eye, to tune into the carrying beam."

The phenomenology of the propulsion as observed from Earth: an Elohim craft moves through the visible color spectrum (red through blue) and then disappears as it transitions out of the optical window into the faster carrying rays. The substantial witness accounts of UFOs shifting through colors before disappearing are, on the source reading, accurate observations of this propulsion-transition process.

The substantial physics objection — that special relativity prohibits material objects from exceeding lightspeed — is addressed by the variable-lightspeed cosmology treated above. On the source's account, the speed of light is not a universal constant but varies with the local density of the sub-quantum medium; superluminal travel relative to the local lightspeed at the propulsion-craft's coupling frequency is therefore not the impossible-physics violation it initially appears to be.

The travel time at the original 7× lightspeed: approximately two months for the ~1-light-year journey. By 1975 (the time of Vorilhon's second contact), the propulsion technology had substantially advanced; the same journey could be completed in "a matter of moments."

The intergalactic ship near Earth

The home planet itself is not the alliance's principal operational base near Earth. The source specifies that an intergalactic ship operates as the permanent alliance base near Earth:

"No, I returned to an intergalactic ship which we use as a base and which stays constantly close to the Earth."

The intergalactic ship's specifications:

  • Permanent stationing near Earth, with continuous operational presence
  • Nine permanent personnel: seven Elohim representatives (one from each of the home planet's seven provinces) plus two Elohim responsible for the vessel itself
  • Rotating Council representation: the seven provincial representatives serve on rotation, with substantial home-planet communication maintained across the rotation periods

The intergalactic ship is the operational interface between the home planet and the Earth-based alliance work. The substantial direct alliance-human contacts (the prophetic missions, the contact events, the Vorilhon contact) operate through ship-based personnel rather than through direct home-planet travel for each event.

Earth-based alliance installations

In addition to the intergalactic ship, the source registers substantial alliance installations on Earth itself:

"The creators had bases — in the Andes, in the Himalayas, in Greece where Greek mythology also contains important testimonies, as well as in the Buddhist and Islamic religions and among the Mormons."

The principal Earth-based installations:

  • Andean — the substantial alliance presence in South America that produced the substantial Andean cultural-religious content (Viracocha tradition, Inca cultural-knowledge tradition, the broader pre-Columbian Andean religious heritage)
  • Himalayan — the substantial alliance presence in the Tibetan Plateau and Himalayan region producing the substantial Hindu, Buddhist, and broader Asian cultural-religious content
  • Greek — the substantial alliance presence in the Eastern Mediterranean producing the substantial Greek mythological and philosophical content
  • Various other locations — including the locations associated with the Buddhist, Islamic, and Mormon traditions

The Earth-based installations operated principally during the early phases of the alliance work and during the various subsequent prophetic missions. The substantial contemporary alliance presence on Earth is principally orbital (the intergalactic ship) rather than terrestrial-base.

Relationship to the Planet of the Eternals

The source content allows two substantive readings of the home planet's relationship to "the Planet of the Eternals." The two readings need careful articulation.

Single-planet reading

The single-planet reading treats the home planet and the Planet of the Eternals as the same world. Yahweh's statement to Vorilhon in 1975:

"In my first message I told you of a residence located on our planet where people from Earth can continue to live thanks to the scientific secret of eternity that is based on a single cell. Among those people are Jesus, Moses, Elijah and so on. This residence is, in fact, very large, since it is an entire planet where the members of the Council of the Eternals live as well."

The natural reading of this statement: the residence (where Vorilhon was taken in 1975) is the entire home planet, with the Council of the Eternals' members living on the same world as the broader home-civilization population. The substantive content: when the source refers to "the Planet of the Eternals" or "the planet where we live," both designations refer to the same world.

The single-planet reading is substantially supported by the source's direct statements. Vorilhon describes his visit as a visit to "our planet" (Yahweh's planet), with no indication of multiple distinct worlds in the same system. The source describes the home-civilization population (7 billion) and the Council of the Eternals population (9,100) as living on the same world.

Two-planet reading

The two-planet reading treats the Planet of the Eternals as a second smaller planet in the same home stellar system, distinct from the main home planet. The corpus timeline.epub engagement articulates this reading explicitly:

"The prophets are, as the corpus has described in earlier chapters, currently resurrected and living on the Planet of the Eternals — the second smaller planet in the alliance's home system, where the approximately nine thousand eternals (alliance officers and selected humans) live in conditions of practical immortality maintained by the alliance's cloning and memory-transfer technology."

The two-planet reading proposes that the Planet of the Eternals is a smaller dedicated world reserved for the Council of the Eternals and the resurrected humans, while the broader home-civilization population (7 billion) lives on a larger main home planet in the same stellar system. The substantive content: when the source refers to "the planet where we live" in conversations with Vorilhon, the reference may be to the Planet of the Eternals specifically rather than to the broader home planet, with Vorilhon never having visited the main home planet in his 1975 contact.

The two-planet reading has support in the source's substantial emphasis on the eternals' residence as a substantial paradise distinct from ordinary life. type
Extrasolar planet; political-civilizational center of the Elohim alliance that conducted the genetic-engineering project producing terrestrial life
principal_source
Claude Vorilhon, Message from the Designers (Tagman Press, 2005), consolidating Le Livre qui dit la vérité (1974), Les Extra-Terrestres m'ont emmené sur leur planète (1975), and Accueillir les Extra-Terrestres (1979)
location_from_home_star
236,000 parasangs (~70.8 billion kilometers); home star substantially larger than the Sun
location_from_earth
30,000,000 parasangs (~9 trillion kilometers, approximately 1 light-year); subject to the substantial astronomical-inconsistency objection the source itself addresses through deliberate-obfuscation and variable-lightspeed cosmology
parasang_unit
Source-redefined unit: distance light travels in one second (~300,000 kilometers); etymologically borrowed from ancient Persian linear-distance unit but redefined as celestial-distance unit in the source's vocabulary
environment
Mild temperature; lush vegetation with exotic flora; azure seas; agricultural-ecological character described as paradisal
civilization
~7 billion inhabitants; seven provinces; official language closely resembling ancient Hebrew
council_of_eternals
~700 Elohim members + ~8,400 resurrected humans from Earth; presided by Yahweh (first individual successfully cloned-and-memory-transferred ~25,000 years ago)
economy
Post-scarcity; atom-based energy; biological robots performing mechanical labor; intellectual-creative activity as principal human pursuit
travel_technology
Atom-based propulsion at 7× lightspeed at time of Vorilhon contact (1973-1975); journey to Earth ~2 months; subsequent technological advance to substantially shorter journey times
relationship_to_planet_of_eternals
Source content allows two readings: single-planet identification (the home planet IS the Planet of the Eternals) or two-planet system (the Planet of the Eternals is a second smaller planet in the same stellar system); the corpus registers both readings as compatible with the source material
distinguished_from
The intergalactic ship near Earth (Elohim operational base with seven Elohim representatives plus two ship-responsible Elohim); Earth-based Elohim installations (the Andean, Himalayan, Greek, and other regional bases); Mormon Kolob (which may or may not refer to the same entity, per the Tvedtnes hypothesis)
status_in_framework
Principal extraterrestrial location in the corpus framework; the political-civilizational center of the alliance that conducts the Earth project; the residence of the Council of the Eternals; the destination for the resurrected prophets and qualifying humans

目次

    Wheel of Heaven は、古代世界の創造伝承——ラエル派、聖書、メソポタミア、その他——を、ただひとつの作業仮説を通して読む、開かれた多言語の読解です。すなわち、エロヒムと呼ばれる存在は、よそから来た小さな先進的な人間の文明であり、ここに到来し、地球上の生命を設計し、宗教的諸文献にその記録を残した、という仮説です。この読解は歳差周期の十二の時代にわたって広がります。二万二千年前のプロジェクトの始まりから、現在の開示の時代を経て、次の大いなる年が開く長い未来へと続きます。本サイトはオープンで、典拠を明示し、作業を検証できるよう構築されています。
