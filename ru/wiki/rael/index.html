Раэль

Раэль (урожд. Клод Ворийон, 1946) — основатель раэлианства, сообщающий о прямой трансмиссии от Элохимов, полученной в 1973 и 1975 годах. В корпусе Wheel of Heaven он прочитывается как последний документированный пророк элохимической линии, в преемственности с Моисеем, Иисусом и Мухаммадом.

Raël, born Claude Vorilhon on September 30, 1946 in Vichy, France, is the French former motor-sports journalist who reported a contact with an officer of the Elohim alliance at the Puy-de-Lassolas volcanic crater near Clermont-Ferrand on the morning of December 13, 1973. The contact inaugurated the Aquarian-age prophetic mission and produced the body of teaching subsequently published as Le Livre qui dit la vérité (The Book Which Tells the Truth, 1974), Les Extra-Terrestres m'ont emmené sur leur planète (Extra-Terrestrials Took Me to Their Planet, 1975/76), and Accueillir les Extra-terrestres (Let's Welcome the Extra-terrestrials, 1979) — collectively the principal Raëlian source material that the Wheel of Heaven corpus engages with. The figure subsequently founded the International Raëlian Movement (1976) and across the subsequent five decades has served as the institutional and public face of the broader embassy project and the Aquarian-age program the alliance has proposed for humanity.

Within the corpus's broader framework, Raël occupies a specific position in the cumulative prophetic tradition. The corpus reads Raël as the last of approximately forty prophets sent by the alliance across human history, with each prophet appropriate to the specific cultural and temporal moment at which they appeared and each contributing to the cumulative preparation of humanity for the Aquarian-age revelation. The earlier prophets — Abraham, Moses, the Hebrew prophetic tradition, Buddha, Zarathustra, Jesus, Muhammad, and various others — operated within the indirect-contact framework that the alliance adopted following the Aries-age discovery (treated more fully in the Cosmic Chain entry). The Aquarian-age prophet, on the corpus's framing, had to be a figure of substantially different character than the earlier prophets — a figure drawn from the cultural mainstream of late-twentieth-century secular modernity rather than from any traditionally religious context, positioned to deliver the alliance's message to a scientifically mature humanity that could evaluate the content on its rational merits rather than receiving it through the established religious frameworks of the prior ages. Raël's specific biographical features — the French motor-sports journalist with no prior religious background, the cultural ordinariness, the modern Western sensibility, the absence of any traditional religious lineage — are read by the corpus as deliberate features of the alliance's selection rather than as incidental biographical details.

The figure's identification as the Maitreya of Buddhist tradition is theologically substantial. The source explicitly identifies Raël with the anticipated future Buddha of the Buddhist tradition, with the published anthology The Maitreya (collecting Raël's teachings across the post-1973 decades) making the identification operationally explicit. The identification connects Raël to the broader anticipated-return traditions across world religions — the Buddhist Maitreya, the Christian projected Second Coming, the Islamic Mahdi, the Hindu Kalki avatar, the Zoroastrian Saoshyant, and various others (treated more fully in the Embassy entry's Comparative observations). The framework's reading is that these various anticipated-return traditions across world religions preserve fragmentary memory of the same projected Aquarian-age event, with each tradition preserving the memory in its own cultural-religious framing. Raël is, on this reading, the specific contemporary figure these traditions have been anticipating across the previous ages.

The figure's biographical content is substantially documented through contemporary record — unlike the earlier prophets whose biographical record is heavily mediated through religious tradition. Raël's own autobiographical statements (principally in Extra-Terrestrials Took Me to Their Planet's "My Life Until the First Encounter" section and in subsequent published material), the International Raëlian Movement's institutional records, contemporary journalistic treatments across the past five decades, and substantial academic engagement (Susan Palmer's principal scholarly studies; Massimo Introvigne's CESNUR work; James R. Lewis's edited volumes; various other scholarly engagements) provide a substantially documented record from which the corpus's treatment can draw. The biographical record is contested in specific respects — particularly the December 13, 1973 contact event itself, which mainstream scholarship treats as either authentic religious experience, deliberate religious construction, or some combination, with no scholarly consensus — but the broader biographical outline is well-documented.

The reading is interpretive. Within mainstream scholarship, Raël is treated principally as the founder of a contemporary new-religious-movement (the Raëlian Movement or Raëlism), with substantial scholarly engagement focusing on the movement's specific doctrinal and organizational features, its historical development, its relationship to broader UFO-religion traditions, and its various controversies. Within mainstream theological tradition, Raël is broadly not engaged with directly — the figure does not occupy a recognized position within established Jewish, Christian, Islamic, Buddhist, or other religious frameworks, and the various religious traditions have either ignored Raël or treated him as a problematic figure outside legitimate prophetic tradition. Within the corpus's framework, Raël occupies the specific position the source material establishes — the last of the cumulative prophetic tradition, the Aquarian-age prophet selected for the specific cultural-temporal moment, the founder of the institutional movement that serves as the vehicle for the broader Aquarian-age preparation. The corpus registers the gap between the source-material framing and mainstream-scholarly treatment honestly, treating Raël's prophetic status as a corpus-internal claim rather than as a scholarly consensus position.

Etymology and naming

The figure has two principal designations operating across the source material and the corpus's broader treatment.

"Claude Vorilhon" — the birth name

The birth name Claude Vorilhon is the figure's legal name and the name under which his pre-1973 life unfolded. The name continues to be the legal-civil name across the subsequent decades, though it is used relatively rarely in the Raëlian movement's institutional materials and in subsequent public engagement.

The source material registers the birth name explicitly: the Foreword of the 2005 Message from the Designers English edition opens with "The author of these unique writings was born Claude Vorilhon in France in September 1946. He changed his name to Rael, which means 'messenger' in Hebrew, after meeting a human being from another planet."

"Raël" — the prophetic name

The prophetic name Raël (sometimes transliterated Rael without the diacritical mark) was given to Vorilhon by the alliance officer at the December 13, 1973 contact. The source material provides several distinct etymological glosses:

  • "Messenger" — the simplest gloss, drawing on the Hebrew רעאל (sometimes treated as related to the Hebrew root suggesting messenger-or-shepherd functions)
  • "Light of the Elohim" — alternative gloss
  • "Ambassador of the Elohim" — alternative gloss

The source material's specific framing: the new name marks the transition from the pre-1973 private person to the post-1973 commissioned messenger. The pattern is consistent with earlier alliance operations — Abram becoming Abraham at the Taurus-age alliance encounter (Genesis 17:5), Jacob becoming Israel after the Genesis 32 nocturnal encounter, Simon becoming Peter (Petros) at Jesus's Piscean-age commissioning. The new name marks the formal commissioning of the prophet at the moment of selection.

Other titles within the Raëlian tradition

Several other titles operate within the Raëlian institutional framework:

  • "Guide of Guides" (Guide des Guides) — Raël's principal institutional title within the Raëlian movement, signifying his position as the senior figure within the broader hierarchy of Guides (the Raëlian movement's ordained leadership)
  • "The Maitreya" or "the Maitreya from the West" — the source's identification of Raël with the Buddhist anticipated-future-Buddha tradition
  • "The last of the prophets" — the source's specific framing of Raël's position within the cumulative prophetic tradition
  • "Our Beloved Prophet" — devotional designation used within the Raëlian movement

Corpus-internal usage

The Wheel of Heaven corpus uses Raël as the principal designation across most contexts, with Claude Vorilhon used principally for biographical-historical references to the pre-1973 period and Vorilhon/Raël as the explicit dual designation in contexts where the distinction is operationally relevant. The corpus avoids the various devotional designations (the Maitreya, our Beloved Prophet) in favor of the operationally neutral Raël.

Conventional understanding

The mainstream scholarly and broader cultural treatment of Raël has produced substantial engagement across the past five decades.

Mainstream new-religious-movement scholarship

Mainstream new-religious-movement (NRM) scholarship has produced the most substantial sustained scholarly engagement with Raël and the Raëlian movement. The principal figures:

Susan J. Palmer is the principal long-term scholarly observer of the Raëlian movement. Palmer's Aliens Adored: Raël's UFO Religion (Rutgers University Press, 2004) is the principal book-length scholarly treatment, drawing on extensive ethnographic fieldwork including direct observation of Raëlian seminars, interviews with Raël and senior movement figures, and substantial documentary research. Palmer's broader subsequent work has continued to engage the Raëlian movement across the post-2004 period.

Massimo Introvigne and the Center for Studies on New Religions (CESNUR) have produced substantial scholarly engagement with the Raëlian movement, including various conference papers, journal articles, and Introvigne's own broader work on new-religious-movements. Introvigne's general approach is sympathetic-scholarly — engaging seriously with the movement's claims while maintaining scholarly objectivity.

James R. Lewis has edited several substantial collections on UFO religions including The Gods Have Landed: New Religions from Other Worlds (SUNY Press, 1995) and various subsequent volumes that include substantial engagement with the Raëlian movement.

George D. Chryssides has produced scholarly engagement with the Raëlian movement within his broader work on new-religious-movements and contemporary religious diversity.

The mainstream NRM scholarship generally treats Raël as a sincere religious figure rather than as a deliberate fraud — recognizing Raël as someone who has consistently and across five decades operated according to the framework he established in the 1973-1974 source material — while not endorsing the framework's specific historical claims about the December 13, 1973 contact event.

Academic UFO-religion studies

The broader academic UFO-religion studies field has produced substantial engagement with the Raëlian movement.

Christopher Partridge in his various works on contemporary spirituality and UFO religions has produced substantial engagement with the Raëlian movement, including the UFO Religions edited volume (Routledge, 2003).

Diana G. Tumminia has produced substantial scholarly work on UFO religions, including Alien Worlds: Social and Religious Dimensions of Extraterrestrial Contact (Syracuse University Press, 2007), which engages substantially with the Raëlian movement.

Mikael Rothstein has produced substantial engagement with the Raëlian movement within his broader work on contemporary religion in Scandinavia.

Various conference and journal engagements across the Nova Religio journal, the various CESNUR conferences, and broader academic religious-studies venues have produced substantial scholarly material on the Raëlian movement.

Psychological-anthropological studies of contact experiences

The broader psychological-anthropological literature on contact experiences engages with phenomenologically similar material across multiple cultural frameworks.

John E. Mack's Abduction: Human Encounters with Aliens (Charles Scribner's Sons, 1994) and Passport to the Cosmos: Human Transformation and Alien Encounters (Crown, 1999) provided substantial Harvard-based academic engagement with contact-experience material, treating reported experiences as phenomenologically real without committing to specific ontological claims about their nature.

Various subsequent psychological and anthropological treatments have produced substantial engagement with contact-experience material, with various approaches ranging from skeptical-pathologizing (treating contact experiences as psychiatric phenomena) through neutral-phenomenological (treating them as experiences whose ontological status is undecided) to sympathetic-engaged (treating them as authentic encounters with non-human intelligence).

Journalistic and biographical treatments

Substantial journalistic engagement with Raël has produced extensive contemporary documentary record.

Mainstream journalistic treatments across the 1970s-2020s period have produced substantial coverage in Le Monde, Le Figaro, The Guardian, The New York Times, various French-language press, and broader international media. The journalistic treatments have generally been skeptical-critical, focusing on movement controversies (the 2002 Clonaid claims, the original swastika symbol, sexually-themed practices) and on broader questions about the movement's institutional development.

Sympathetic biographical treatments have been principally produced through Raëlian-movement institutional channels and through individual sympathizers, with less mainstream-scholarly impact.

Critical biographical treatments have included various ex-Raëlian accounts and broader anti-cult organizational engagement, particularly through the French anti-cult organizations (UNADFI, MIVILUDES) that have at various points classified the Raëlian movement as a problematic religious group.

The relationship to the corpus's framework

The corpus's framework treats the substantial mainstream scholarly engagement with Raël as the specific contemporary academic context within which the figure operates, while engaging the source-material content directly rather than mediating it through the various scholarly approaches. The corpus's specific position is that mainstream scholarship has produced substantial engagement with the movement and its institutional development, but has produced limited engagement with the content of the source-material teaching at the operational level — the framework's specific treatment of Genesis 1, the cosmic-political reconstruction, the precessional-age framework, the broader cosmological content. The corpus's distinctive contribution is engagement with this content rather than with the broader movement's institutional development.

In primary sources

The primary-source material on Raël is substantial and contemporary, comprising Raël's own autobiographical writings, the source-material teaching delivered at the contacts, the Raëlian movement's institutional records, and various subsequent published materials.

Raël's autobiographical material

The principal autobiographical material is preserved in Extra-Terrestrials Took Me to Their Planet (1975/76), particularly in the opening section "My Life Until the First Encounter."

Childhood and family background. Vorilhon was born in Vichy, France on September 30, 1946 — the same year the source identifies as "the first year of the new era," with the broader 1945-1946 cluster of events (treated more fully in the Apocalypse and Atomic Threshold entries) marking the Aquarian-age opening. The source treats this birth date as not coincidental: "It is not by chance that you were born in 1946." The Aquarian-age prophet had to be born at the age's beginning, positioned to receive the alliance's contact at the specific point in his adult life when the contact was scheduled to occur.

Childhood UFO experience. Vorilhon's autobiographical material includes a childhood UFO experience over Ambert (a town near Clermont-Ferrand) — a prefigurement of the subsequent direct contact, in the source's framing.

The "Pope of the Druids" episode. Vorilhon's autobiographical material includes an early-life encounter with a figure subsequently identified as having Druid-tradition associations, with the source framing the episode as part of the broader preparation for the prophetic mission.

Early literary and poetic interests. Vorilhon's pre-1973 autobiographical material includes substantial literary and poetic interest, with various early writings preserved in the autobiographical sections.

Motor-sports journalism career. The principal pre-1973 career was as a motor-sports journalist. Vorilhon founded the specialist motor-sports magazine Auto Pop in 1970, drawing on his lifelong interest in motor racing (with childhood aspirations of becoming a racing driver in the tradition of Juan Manuel Fangio). The magazine provided Vorilhon's professional life across the 1970-1973 period, with various race-track involvement and approximately ten racing trophies preserved as personal mementos. The autobiographical material treats the motor-sports period as the specific cultural-professional context within which Vorilhon's pre-1973 life unfolded.

Family situation. At the time of the December 13, 1973 contact, Vorilhon was 27 years old, living in Clermont-Ferrand with his wife and children, and pursuing his motor-sports journalism career.

The December 13, 1973 contact

The principal source material on the December 13, 1973 contact is preserved in The Book Which Tells the Truth (1974), with Raël's own narrative account preserved in the opening "The Encounter" section.

The location and circumstance. The Puy-de-Lassolas volcanic crater is one of the Chaîne des Puys, a north-south line of approximately eighty volcanic features near Clermont-Ferrand in the Auvergne region of central France. The crater is a bowl-shaped depression approximately 100 meters across, accessible by walking path. Vorilhon's autobiographical narrative describes the December 1973 morning as cool, with background mist and gray sky, the crater "almost certainly deserted." Vorilhon had walked up to the crater for what he described as "a breath of fresh air" rather than for any specific religious or contact-seeking purpose.

The craft. The source's specific description: a small flying saucer, approximately 7 meters in diameter and 2.5 meters in height, flat underneath and cone-shaped, with a bright red flashing light on its underside and a bright intermittent white light on top. The craft descended silently to approximately 2 meters above the ground.

The figure. The source's specific description: a humanoid figure approximately 1.20 meters in height, with long dark hair, a beard, pale and slightly olive-tinted skin, large almond-shaped eyes, and a white single-piece suit. The figure communicated in fluent French.

The six-day contact. Across six successive mornings, the figure delivered the body of teaching that Vorilhon subsequently published as The Book Which Tells the Truth. The teaching covered the broader framework — the Elohim civilization, the Genesis-as-genetic-engineering reading, the broader Hebrew Bible content, the parallel humanities, the embassy specifications, the seven new commandments, the Aquarian-age program. The figure identified himself as an officer of the Elohim, with subsequent material identifying the specific Eloha as Yahweh (the president of the Council of the Eternals).

The new name. The figure named Vorilhon as Raël during the six-day contact. The naming ceremony marked the formal commissioning of Vorilhon as the Aquarian-age prophet.

The October 7, 1975 second contact

The principal source material on the October 7, 1975 second contact is preserved in Extra-Terrestrials Took Me to Their Planet (1975/76).

The location. Roc Plat near Brantôme in the Périgord region of southwestern France (some sources place the location more generally in the broader Auvergne or Dordogne region; the source material's specific identification is Roc Plat near Brantôme).

The journey to the Planet of the Eternals. Raël's account describes being taken aboard a substantially larger craft than the December 1973 vessel, and transported to the alliance's home world (the Planet of the Eternals in Raëlian terminology). The journey's specific physics is not detailed in the source material — Raël describes being transported "in a specific manner" without spacesuit or special apparatus, with the journey being substantially shorter than the light-travel time the conventional physics would imply.

The Planet of the Eternals. The source's description includes substantial detail: the home-world setting, the architectural and natural features, the encounters with various previously-existing prophets (Moses, Elijah, Buddha, Jesus, Muhammad, and others — preserved on the home world through the alliance's biotechnology), the specific philosophical and cosmological content delivered across the visit.

The biological-parentage disclosure. During the second contact, Raël was informed of his biological parentage by Yahweh — the president of the Council of the Eternals as Raël's biological father. The disclosure was accompanied by the request that Raël withhold publication of this information for three years, to avoid producing what the source describes as "a serious imbalance" in the broader public reception. The disclosure was eventually published in 1979's Let's Welcome the Extra-terrestrials, the third volume of the principal source material.

The seven new commandments. The seven new commandments (geniocracy, humanitarianism, world government, demilitarization, science as religion, telepathic cultivation, the metaphysical clarifications) were delivered across the second contact, providing the broader political-religious program the alliance has proposed for the Aquarian age.

Subsequent contacts and messages

Several subsequent contacts and telepathic messages are preserved in the source material.

The October 7, 1976 message. Approximately fifty Raëlians were present at La Negrerie near Roc Plat for the first anniversary of the October 7, 1975 contact. A subsequent telepathic message and broader gathering occurred at this anniversary event.

The March 14, 1978 telepathic message. The principal subsequent telepathic message regarding the parallel humanities (treated more fully in the Cosmic Competition entry). The message established Earth's specific cooperative role with respect to the parallel humanities.

The December 13, 1997 telepathic message. The principal subsequent telepathic message regarding the embassy relocation (treated more fully in the Embassy entry). The message authorized the Raëlian movement to seek extraterritorial status for the embassy from "all nations on Earth" rather than only Israel.

The published works

Raël's published works across the 1974-2005 period (with subsequent works through the present) constitute the principal documentary record of the Aquarian-age prophetic mission.

The principal works:

  • Le Livre qui dit la vérité (The Book Which Tells the Truth, 1974) — the first volume, recording the December 13, 1973 contact and the principal teaching delivered across the six-day contact
  • Les Extra-Terrestres m'ont emmené sur leur planète (Extra-Terrestrials Took Me to Their Planet, 1975/76) — the second volume, recording the October 7, 1975 contact and the visit to the Planet of the Eternals
  • Accueillir les Extra-terrestres (Let's Welcome the Extra-terrestrials, 1979) — the third volume, including the previously-withheld biological-parentage disclosure and substantial subsequent material
  • La Géniocratie (Geniocracy, 1978) — substantial elaboration of the geniocracy commandment
  • La Méditation Sensuelle (Sensual Meditation, 1980) — meditation practices designed by the Elohim for awakening the senses
  • Oui au clonage humain (Yes to Human Cloning, 2000) — engagement with the cloning technology that became substantially relevant following the 1997 Dolly-the-sheep cloning
  • Le Maitreya (anthology) — collection of Raël's teachings across the post-1973 decades

The combined works have been translated into approximately thirty languages, with over two million copies sold worldwide as of the 2005 Message from the Designers English edition's publication.

The figure's content

Pre-prophetic life (1946-1973)

The pre-prophetic life is documented principally through Raël's autobiographical material.

Childhood and adolescence (1946-1962). Vorilhon was born in Vichy, France on September 30, 1946. The source material's specific framing places the birth date at the opening of the Aquarian age — 1946 being identified by the source as "the first year of the new era." The childhood and adolescent period was, by Vorilhon's own account, ordinary in most respects, with substantial early literary and poetic interests, the childhood UFO experience over Ambert, the early encounter with the figure subsequently identified as the "Pope of the Druids," and various other prefiguring episodes.

Early career (1962-1970). Vorilhon's pre-1970 career was variable, with various professional engagements before the establishment of the motor-sports journalism focus. The autobiographical material registers various early professional pursuits without substantial emphasis.

Motor-sports journalism (1970-1973). Vorilhon founded Auto Pop magazine in 1970, with the specialist motor-sports focus reflecting his lifelong interest in motor racing. The 1970-1973 period was the principal pre-prophetic professional period, with various race-track involvement (approximately ten racing trophies preserved) and the establishment of Vorilhon's professional identity as a motor-sports journalist.

Family life. Vorilhon married and had children during the pre-prophetic period, with the family residence in Clermont-Ferrand serving as the broader life context within which the December 13, 1973 contact occurred.

The 1973-1975 inaugural contact period

The two-year period between the December 13, 1973 first contact and the October 7, 1975 second contact comprised the principal inaugural period of the Aquarian-age prophetic mission.

The principal events of 1974. The Book Which Tells the Truth was published in early 1974, providing the first publicly available record of the December 13, 1973 contact. The publication produced substantial initial reception within the broader French intellectual-cultural context, with various journalistic and broader public engagement.

The first public talks. Raël began public lecturing in 1974, with the first public talks providing the operational basis for the subsequent institutional development.

The MADECH founding (1974). The first organizational body, MADECH (Mouvement pour l'Accueil des Élohim Créateurs de l'Humanité — "Movement for Welcoming the Elohim, Creators of Humanity"), was founded in 1974 to provide institutional vehicle for the broader teaching dissemination. MADECH was the predecessor organization to the subsequent International Raëlian Movement.

The 1975 second contact. The October 7, 1975 second contact at Roc Plat provided the substantial elaboration of the inaugural teaching, the visit to the Planet of the Eternals, the biological-parentage disclosure, and the seven new commandments.

The 1976-1990 institutional development period

The period 1976-1990 comprised the principal institutional development of the Raëlian movement.

The 1976 founding of the International Raëlian Movement. The International Raëlian Movement (IRM) was founded in 1976 as the successor and broader-scope organization following MADECH. The IRM became the principal institutional vehicle for the broader Raëlian project.

The 1976 publication of Extra-Terrestrials Took Me to Their Planet. The second volume of the principal source material was published in 1976, providing the documentary record of the October 7, 1975 contact and the broader subsequent material.

The 1978 publication of Geniocracy. The substantial elaboration of the first new commandment was published in 1978.

The 1979 publication of Let's Welcome the Extra-terrestrials. The third volume of the principal source material was published in 1979, including the previously-withheld biological-parentage disclosure.

The 1980s expansion. The Raëlian movement expanded substantially across the 1980s, with the publication of Sensual Meditation (1980), the establishment of national branches across multiple countries, and the broader institutional development.

The 1990 symbol modification. In 1990, as a sign of special feelings toward the people of Israel and out of respect for victims of the Nazi swastika, the Elohim agreed to Raël's suggestion to modify the original Raëlian Symbol of Infinity in Western branches. The central swastika (which means "well-being" in Sanskrit and represents infinity in time) was replaced with a galaxy-shaped swirl. The original symbol remained in use in Asia, where the swastika appears in Buddhist temples without the Western political-historical associations.

The 1991-2005 embassy-engagement period

The period 1991-2005 comprised the principal embassy-engagement period, with the Israeli negotiations and broader diplomatic engagement (treated more fully in the Embassy entry).

The 1991-1997 Israeli engagement. The principal Israeli requests across the 1991-1997 period (treated under the Embassy entry).

The 1997 publication of Yes to Human Cloning (in original French; English edition 2000). Raël's engagement with the cloning question following the 1997 Dolly-the-sheep cloning announcement.

The 1997 Clonaid initiation. Raël initiated the Clonaid project to bring attention to the human cloning question. The project was subsequently transferred to independent scientific leadership under Brigitte Boisselier.

The December 13, 1997 message. The telepathic message authorizing the embassy relocation (treated under the Embassy entry).

The 2002 Clonaid claim. On December 27, 2002, Brigitte Boisselier (Clonaid president, Raëlian Bishop) announced at a press conference in Hollywood, Florida, that Clonaid had assisted the birth of the first cloned human baby, nicknamed "Eve." The claim has not been independently scientifically verified across the subsequent two decades, and substantial scientific and journalistic skepticism has been registered. Clonaid subsequently announced additional cloned births that have similarly not been independently verified. The 2002 announcement produced substantial international media attention and substantially elevated the broader public profile of the Raëlian movement.

The 2005 Message from the Designers publication. The English-language consolidated edition of the principal three source-material volumes was published in 2005, providing the principal contemporary English-language access point to the Raëlian source material.

The post-2005 period

The post-2005 period has comprised the continuing development of the Raëlian movement and the broader embassy project.

The continuing institutional development. The International Raëlian Movement has continued to develop across the post-2005 period, with continuing institutional expansion in multiple countries, continuing seminars and educational events, and continuing broader public engagement.

The 2015 UN Optional Protocol Concerning Embassies for Extraterrestrials. The submission of the formal diplomatic framework to the United Nations.

The 2021 establishment of the Alliance for Extraterrestrial Diplomatic Contact. The international non-profit organization of politicians, diplomats, and government officials.

The 2024 Memorandum of Collaboration. The undisclosed national government's signing of the Memorandum of Collaboration with the Raëlian movement.

Raël's continuing role. Across the post-2005 period, Raël has continued in the institutional role of Guide of Guides, while progressively transferring substantial operational responsibility to senior Raëlian movement figures (notably Daniel Turcotte for the embassy project, Brigitte Boisselier for Clonaid-related work, and various other senior Guides for specific operational areas). The 2005 Message from the Designers preface notes Raël's specific recognition that "the International Raelian Movement... has become self-sustaining and could now function perfectly well without me," registering the operational maturation of the institutional movement.

The figure's specific role within the cumulative prophetic tradition

The corpus's framework reads Raël as the last of approximately forty prophets sent by the alliance across human history. The specific position involves several distinctive features:

The Aquarian-age commissioning. Raël's commissioning is the specific Aquarian-age commissioning, with the prophet selected for the specific cultural-temporal moment of late-twentieth-century scientific maturity.

The cumulative tradition's culmination. Raël's mission is the culminating prophetic mission of the cumulative tradition spanning the previous ages — the various Hebrew prophets, the Buddhist tradition's principal figures, the Hellenistic and broader pre-Christian figures, Jesus, Muhammad, and the various other principal prophetic figures across world religions. The cumulative tradition prepared humanity across the previous ages for the specific Aquarian-age revelation that Raël's mission delivers.

The direct-articulation distinctiveness. Unlike the earlier prophets, who delivered their teaching in forms substantially mediated by their cultural-religious contexts, Raël's teaching is delivered in substantially direct form — without the mythological-symbolic apparatus of the earlier traditions, without the priestly-institutional mediation of subsequent religious tradition, accessible to a scientifically mature humanity that can evaluate the content on its rational merits.

The specific cultural ordinariness. Raël's pre-prophetic biographical features (the French motor-sports journalist with no prior religious background) are read by the corpus as deliberate features of the alliance's selection. The Aquarian-age prophet had to be drawn from cultural ordinariness rather than from established religious tradition, to ensure the message would not be received through the inherited religious frameworks of the prior ages.

The biological-parentage feature. Raël's biological parentage by Yahweh is the specific feature distinguishing his prophetic position from earlier prophets — though earlier prophets in various cultural traditions have also been identified with semi-divine parentage (Jesus's tradition, various Hellenistic hero-traditions, various other parallel cases). The framework registers the biological-parentage feature without making it the principal content of the prophetic identification.

The institutional founder role

Raël's institutional role as founder of the International Raëlian Movement is operationally distinctive. The principal features:

The Guide of Guides position. Raël functions as the senior figure within the broader hierarchy of Raëlian Guides, with substantial operational and pastoral responsibilities across the institutional movement.

The institutional vehicle for the embassy project. Distinguishing from adjacent figures

Raël vs. the earlier prophets

The earlier prophets (Moses, Jesus, Muhammad, Buddha, Zarathustra, the Hebrew prophetic tradition, various others) operated within the indirect-contact framework that the alliance adopted following the Aries-age discovery. Raël operates within the broader Aquarian-age direct-contact framework — a substantially different operational context.

The relationship is one of cumulative-tradition-and-culmination. The earlier prophets prepared humanity across the previous ages for the specific Aquarian-age revelation that Raël's mission delivers; Raël's mission is the cumulative tradition's culmination rather than a parallel addition to it.

Raël vs. the broader UFO contactee tradition

The mid-20th century UFO contactee tradition (Adamski, Klarer, Meier, Andreasson, and various others) preceded Raël's mission and provides one specific cultural-religious context within which the December 13, 1973 contact occurred. The broader contactee tradition has substantial differences from the Raëlian framework — most contactees produced substantially less elaborated teaching, most did not establish substantial institutional movements, most did not produce the systematic textual corpus that Raël has produced.

The relationship is one of broader-cultural-context-and-distinctive-figure. The contactee tradition provides the broader cultural-religious context within which the Raëlian mission operates; Raël is structurally distinctive within this broader context through the systematic teaching, the institutional development, and the specific framework's elaborated content.

Raël vs. mainstream new-religious-movement founders

The various other new-religious-movement founders of the post-1960 period (L. Ron Hubbard, Sun Myung Moon, various others) operated in the same broader cultural-religious moment as Raël. The various NRM founders have substantial common features (institutional founding, doctrinal articulation, controversial reception) and substantial differences (specific doctrinal content, institutional development patterns, controversies).

The relationship is one of shared-cultural-moment-and-distinctive-content. The various NRM founders share the broader cultural-religious moment of late-twentieth-century religious innovation; Raël is structurally distinctive through the specific extraterrestrial-contact framework, the systematic textual corpus, and the broader Aquarian-age framework.

Raël vs. the institutional Raëlian movement

Raël as figure and the broader Raëlian movement as institutional entity are operationally distinct. Raël is the founder and continuing senior figure; the broader movement is the institutional vehicle that Raël founded and continues to lead but that has developed substantial operational infrastructure beyond Raël's personal involvement.

The relationship is one of founder-and-institutional-development. The detailed treatment of the broader institutional movement lives in the Raëlism entry; the Raël entry treats the figure specifically.

Modern reinterpretations

Mainstream new-religious-movement scholarship

The principal mainstream scholarly engagement with Raël has been within new-religious-movement (NRM) studies.

Susan J. Palmer's Aliens Adored: Raël's UFO Religion (Rutgers University Press, 2004) is the principal book-length scholarly treatment. Palmer's approach is sympathetic-ethnographic, drawing on extensive direct fieldwork including substantial interview material with Raël and senior Raëlian movement figures. The book treats Raël as a sincere religious figure while remaining scholarly-objective regarding the framework's specific historical claims. Palmer's broader subsequent work has continued to engage the Raëlian movement across the post-2004 period.

Massimo Introvigne and the Center for Studies on New Religions (CESNUR) have produced substantial scholarly engagement with Raël across various conference papers, journal articles, and broader CESNUR institutional output. Introvigne's general approach is sympathetic-scholarly, treating Raël seriously as a religious figure while maintaining scholarly objectivity.

James R. Lewis has edited several substantial collections engaging with the Raëlian movement, including The Gods Have Landed: New Religions from Other Worlds (SUNY Press, 1995) and various subsequent volumes.

George D. Chryssides has produced scholarly engagement with the Raëlian movement within his broader work on new-religious-movements.

The mainstream NRM scholarship's general position is to treat Raël as a sincere religious figure rather than as a deliberate fraud — recognizing the consistent five-decade pattern of behavior that suggests authentic religious commitment rather than deliberate construction — while not endorsing the framework's specific historical claims about the December 13, 1973 contact event.

Academic UFO-religion studies

The broader academic UFO-religion studies field has produced substantial engagement with Raël.

Christopher Partridge's edited volume UFO Religions (Routledge, 2003) and his broader work on contemporary spirituality have produced substantial engagement with the Raëlian movement.

Diana G. Tumminia's Alien Worlds: Social and Religious Dimensions of Extraterrestrial Contact (Syracuse University Press, 2007) provides substantial sociological engagement with the broader UFO-religion phenomenon, including substantial Raëlian content.

Mikael Rothstein has produced substantial engagement with the Raëlian movement within his broader work on contemporary religion in Scandinavia.

Robert S. Ellwood and Catherine Wessinger have produced substantial engagement with UFO religions within their broader work on alternative-religious-movements.

The academic UFO-religion studies generally treat Raël as one figure within a broader contemporary religious phenomenon, with the specific Raëlian framework being one specific articulation of the broader UFO-religion category.

Psychological-anthropological studies of contact experiences

The broader psychological-anthropological literature on contact experiences provides relevant context for Raël's specific December 13, 1973 contact event.

John E. Mack's Abduction: Human Encounters with Aliens (Charles Scribner's Sons, 1994) and Passport to the Cosmos: Human Transformation and Alien Encounters (Crown, 1999) provided substantial Harvard-based academic engagement with contact-experience material. Mack's general approach was phenomenological-respectful — treating reported contact experiences as phenomenologically real without committing to specific ontological claims about their nature.

Various subsequent psychological and anthropological treatments have engaged contact-experience material across multiple methodological frameworks. The principal current frameworks include skeptical-pathologizing (treating contact experiences as psychiatric phenomena like sleep paralysis, hypnopompic hallucinations, or dissociative states), neutral-phenomenological (treating contact experiences as subjective experiences whose ontological status is undecided), and sympathetic-engaged (treating contact experiences as authentic encounters with non-human intelligence).

The various frameworks produce substantially different readings of Raël's specific December 13, 1973 contact event — from skeptical readings that treat the event as some specific psychological phenomenon, through neutral readings that suspend ontological judgment, to sympathetic readings that treat the event as authentic. The corpus's framework adopts the broader sympathetic reading while engaging seriously with the alternative interpretive frameworks.

Journalistic and biographical treatments

Substantial journalistic engagement with Raël has produced extensive contemporary documentary record.

Mainstream French-language journalism across Le Monde, Le Figaro, Libération, and various other French publications has produced substantial coverage across the 1970s-2020s period. The general orientation has been skeptical-critical, with substantial engagement with movement controversies (the 2002 Clonaid claims, the original swastika symbol, sexually-themed practices) and broader skepticism regarding the framework's historical claims.

Mainstream English-language journalism across The Guardian, The New York Times, The Washington Post, The Times (UK), and various other publications has produced similar substantial engagement with similar general orientation.

Specialist religious-studies journalism through publications like Religion News Service and various academic-adjacent venues has produced more nuanced engagement, drawing on the broader NRM scholarship's framework rather than principally on the journalistic skeptical-critical orientation.

Anti-cult organizational engagement through the French anti-cult organizations (UNADFI, MIVILUDES) and various international anti-cult organizations has produced substantial critical-hostile material across the past several decades. The French government's engagement with the Raëlian movement has been substantially complicated by the anti-cult organizational pressure.

Sympathetic biographical treatments have been principally produced through Raëlian-movement institutional channels and through individual sympathizers, with less mainstream-scholarly impact.

Critical biographical treatments have included various ex-Raëlian accounts (with various levels of credibility and various specific complaints) and broader anti-cult organizational engagement.

The corpus's specific position

The Wheel of Heaven corpus's position on the modern reinterpretive landscape is one of selective engagement. The corpus engages substantially with mainstream NRM scholarship for the broader institutional context, with the academic UFO-religion studies for the broader cultural context, with the psychological-anthropological literature for the broader contact-experience context, and with the journalistic-biographical material for the documentary record. The corpus's distinctive contribution is engagement with the source-material content directly rather than with the broader mediating frameworks of the various scholarly approaches — engaging with what Yahweh told Raël across the December 1973 and October 1975 contacts, what the embassy specifications imply for the Aquarian-age operational arrangements, what the broader cosmic-competition and cosmic-chain frameworks require for understanding the Aquarian-age developments. The mainstream scholarship has produced substantial engagement with the movement and figure but limited engagement with the content of the source-material teaching at the operational level the corpus's framework develops.

Comparative observations

Within the cumulative prophetic tradition, Raël occupies a specific position that warrants comparative engagement with the principal earlier prophets and with various contemporaneous figures.

Moses (Aries-age comparable prophet)

Moses occupies a structurally comparable position within the Aries-age prophetic tradition. The principal comparative features:

The age-inaugural commissioning. Moses's commissioning at the burning bush (Exodus 3) inaugurated the Aries-age direct-contact period; Raël's commissioning at the Puy-de-Lassolas inaugurated the Aquarian-age direct-contact period. Both commissionings involved direct alliance contact with a previously unremarkable figure, with the figure's subsequent mission being the principal age-defining religious-political project.

The new-name marking. Moses's specific name was preserved without change (though the broader Hebrew tradition has developed substantial subsequent interpretive material on the name's etymology). Raël received a new name from the alliance officer at the December 13, 1973 contact, marking the formal commissioning. The new-name marking is a recurring feature of alliance-prophet selections (Abram→Abraham, Jacob→Israel, Simon→Peter), with Raël's case continuing the pattern.

The detailed-teaching dictation. Moses received the Mosaic Law at Sinai across an extended period of direct contact; Raël received the source-material teaching across the six-day Puy-de-Lassolas contact and the subsequent Roc Plat second contact. Both prophetic missions involved substantial detailed-teaching dictation that subsequently became foundational textual material.

The institutional-founding role. Moses founded the broader Israelite religious-political tradition; Raël founded the International Raëlian Movement. Both founding roles involved substantial subsequent institutional development.

The specific differences. Moses operated within the direct-contact framework that preceded the Aries-age discovery; Raël operates within the Aquarian-age direct-contact framework that follows the discovery. Moses's mission was substantially mediated by the cultural-religious context of the late Bronze Age Hebrew tradition; Raël's mission is substantially mediated by the cultural-secular context of late-twentieth-century scientific modernity.

Jesus (Piscean-age comparable prophet)

Jesus occupies the structurally comparable position within the Piscean-age prophetic tradition. The principal comparative features:

The age-inaugural commissioning. Jesus's commissioning at the Jordan baptism (Mark 1:9-11) inaugurated the Piscean-age prophetic mission; Raël's commissioning inaugurated the Aquarian-age mission. Both commissionings involved alliance-direct identification of the prophet (the Jordan voice; the Puy-de-Lassolas naming).

The biological-parentage feature. The Christian tradition's identification of Jesus as biological son of God / the divine principle has structural parallels with Raël's biological-parentage by Yahweh. The framework reads both cases as instances of the broader pattern in which alliance-age-inaugurating prophets have biological connections to senior alliance figures.

The cumulative-tradition's culminating teacher. Jesus operated as the principal Piscean-age teacher whose teaching produced the cumulative subsequent religious tradition; Raël operates as the cumulative tradition's culminating teacher whose teaching delivers the Aquarian-age framework.

The detailed-textual-corpus production. Jesus's teaching produced the substantial subsequent New Testament corpus through the apostolic transmission; Raël's teaching has produced the substantial Raëlian source-material corpus through Raël's direct authorship across the past five decades.

The specific differences. Jesus's teaching was preserved through the substantially mediated apostolic transmission and subsequent ecclesiastical development; Raël's teaching is preserved through Raël's direct authorship and the substantially less-mediated subsequent transmission. Jesus's mission was substantially mediated by the cultural-religious context of first-century Hellenistic Judaism; Raël's mission is substantially mediated by the cultural-secular context of late-twentieth-century scientific modernity.

Muhammad

Muhammad occupies a specific position within the broader prophetic tradition that warrants comparative engagement.

The age-late commissioning. Muhammad's commissioning at Mount Hira (c. 610 CE) occurred within the Piscean age but at a distinctive cultural-political moment — the broader Arabian peninsula's specific cultural-religious situation provided the operational context for Muhammad's specific mission. Raël's commissioning occurred at the Aquarian-age opening.

The detailed-textual-corpus production. Muhammad's teaching produced the Qur'anic corpus through substantially direct transmission (the Qur'an being substantially preserved as direct transmission of the angelic revelations). Raël's teaching has produced the Raëlian source-material corpus through similarly direct authorship.

The institutional-founding role. Muhammad founded the Islamic religious-political tradition; Raël founded the International Raëlian Movement.

Joseph Smith (Latter-day Saints comparable)

Joseph Smith (1805-1844) occupies a specific position within the broader prophetic-founder tradition that warrants comparative engagement.

The 19th-century age-late commissioning. Smith's commissioning through the angelic visitations of the 1820s and the subsequent Book of Mormon translation work occurred within the Piscean age's closing centuries — a cultural-religious moment broadly preparatory for the Aquarian-age opening. Raël's commissioning occurred at the Aquarian-age opening.

The detailed-textual-corpus production. Smith's mission produced the Book of Mormon and the broader LDS canonical scripture; Raël's mission has produced the Raëlian source-material corpus.

The institutional-founding role. Smith founded the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints and the broader Mormon religious tradition; Raël founded the International Raëlian Movement.

The new-religious-movement scholarly category. Both Smith and Raël occupy the new-religious-movement scholarly category — figures who founded religious traditions outside the principal mainstream traditions of their respective historical moments.

Modern channeling/contactee figures

The broader 20th-century channeling/contactee tradition provides the immediate cultural-religious context within which Raël's mission operates. The principal figures:

George Adamski (1891-1965) is the principal early UFO-contactee figure, with reported contacts beginning in 1952 and substantial subsequent literary output (Flying Saucers Have Landed, 1953; Inside the Space Ships, 1955; others). Adamski's specific framework involved alleged contacts with Venusian, Martian, and Saturnian figures, with substantial subsequent debunking but continuing influence on the broader contactee tradition.

Elizabeth Klarer (1910-1994) was a South African contactee with reported contacts beginning in 1956 and substantial subsequent autobiographical material (Beyond the Light Barrier, 1980).

Billy Meier (Eduard Albert Meier, b. 1937) is a Swiss contactee with reported contacts beginning in 1975 (close in time to Raël's principal contacts). Meier's specific framework involves alleged contacts with Pleiadian and broader extraterrestrial figures, with substantial subsequent literary output and continuing controversy.

Betty Andreasson (b. 1937) is a US contactee whose reported abduction experience beginning 1967 became the principal subject of Raymond E. Fowler's investigative work (The Andreasson Affair, 1979; subsequent volumes), producing substantial subsequent engagement.

Various other contactees have produced substantial parallel material across the post-1947 period, with the broader contactee tradition providing the immediate cultural-religious context for Raël's specific mission.

The framework reads the broader contactee tradition as the immediate cultural-religious context within which Raël's mission operates, with Raël being structurally distinctive within this broader context through the systematic teaching, the institutional development, the specific framework's elaborated content, and the broader connection to the cumulative prophetic tradition that the corpus identifies. The various contactees produced substantially less elaborated teaching, did not establish substantial institutional movements with comparable scope, and did not produce the systematic textual corpus that Raël has produced.

The convergence

The corpus's working position on the comparative-prophetic question is that Raël's specific position within the cumulative prophetic tradition has substantial structural parallels to the earlier age-inaugural prophets (Moses, Jesus) while being distinctively shaped by the specific Aquarian-age operational requirements (direct-articulation rather than mythologically-mediated teaching, cultural-mainstream rather than traditionally-religious figure, institutional-founding within secular rather than religious-traditional context). The broader UFO-contactee tradition provides the immediate cultural-religious context within which Raël's mission operates, with Raël being distinctively positioned within this context through the systematic content and institutional development.

The mainstream scholarly explanation — which generally treats Raël as one figure within a broader new-religious-movement and UFO-religion phenomenon, with the various phenomena explained through standard religious-studies methodologies — captures substantial features of the broader institutional and cultural context but does not engage the specific operational content of the source-material teaching at the level the corpus's framework develops. The framework's specific reading is that Raël's mission is structurally consistent with the broader cumulative prophetic tradition, that the December 13, 1973 contact event is to be read as authentic within the framework's operational reality, and that the subsequent five decades of consistent operational behavior provide substantial evidence for the broader authenticity of the prophetic mission.

Wheel of Heaven — это открытое многоязычное прочтение преданий о сотворении мира древних культур — раэлианских, библейских, месопотамских и других — через единственную рабочую гипотезу: что существа, называемые Элохим, были небольшой развитой человеческой цивилизацией извне, которая прибыла сюда, спроектировала жизнь на Земле и оставила запись в религиозных текстах. Прочтение охватывает двенадцать эпох прецессионного цикла — от начала проекта двадцать две тысячи лет назад до нынешней эпохи раскрытия и далее в долгое будущее, которое открывает следующий Великий год. Сайт свободный, опирающийся на источники, и построен так, чтобы работу можно было проверить.
